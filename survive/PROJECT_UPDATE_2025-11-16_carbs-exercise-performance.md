## Key Research Findings

### Study 1: Pre-Exercise Meal Impact (5K/10K Performance)
- **Design**: Randomized controlled crossover trial, 15 subjects each
- **Finding**: NO DIFFERENCE in performance between high-carb and very low-carb pre-exercise meals
- **Significance**: Challenges "carb loading" paradigm for middle-distance events

### Study 2: 4-Week Ketogenic Adaptation (1-Mile Time Trial)
- **Subjects**: High-end athletes (50+ km/week, lean, high VO2 max)
- **Finding**: No performance deterioration after keto-adaptation
- **Revolutionary**: Fat oxidation rates of **1.5-1.85g/min at 85%+ VO2 max**
- **Context**: Previously thought impossible; highest ever recorded in literature

### Study 3: 12-Week Cycling Study (Middle-Aged Athletes)
- **Design**: Half on keto, half on high-carb Western diet
- **Finding**: EQUAL improvements in VO2 max, lactate threshold, body composition
- **Fat oxidation**: 1.2g/min at 75% VO2 max (traditionally impossible)

---

## Paradigm Shifts Documented

1. **Fuel Source Independence**: Performance not determined by carb vs. fat oxidation
2. **Fat Oxidation Ceiling Shattered**: 1.85g/min vs. old belief of 0.5-0.7g/min max
3. **Glycogen Sparing**: Keto-adapted athletes maintain normal glycogen levels despite low carb intake
4. **Central vs. Peripheral Fatigue**: "Bonking" may be CNS glucose depletion, not muscle glycogen
