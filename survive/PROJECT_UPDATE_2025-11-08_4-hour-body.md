## Key Concepts Integrated

### 1. Minimum Effective Dose (MED)
**Definition**: The smallest intervention that produces desired results.

**Applications**:
- Tactical fitness: What's minimum training for operational readiness?
- Skill acquisition: Minimum practice for proficiency?
- Resource allocation: Highest-leverage preparedness investments?

**Project Impact**: Focuses effort on 20% of activities that deliver 80% of results.

### 2. The Slow-Carb Diet
**Core Protocol**:
- Avoid white carbs
- Same meals repeatedly
- No calorie drinks
- No fruit (except tomato/avocado)
- One cheat day/week

**Preparedness Value**:
- Simple meal planning (reduces decision fatigue)
- Food storage friendly (legumes, canned proteins last years)
- Sustainable energy for manual labor
- No calorie counting (practical in field conditions)
- Psychological sustainability (cheat day prevents deprivation)

### 3. Self-Experimentation Framework
**Methodology**: Treat body as laboratory - test, measure, iterate.

**Applications**:
- Field test survival techniques systematically
- Quantify skill acquisition rates
- Validate gear performance with data
- Document what works in real conditions

### 4. Occam's Protocol (Minimal Strength Training)
**Protocol**: 2 workouts/week using compound movements.

**Value**: Maintains tactical fitness baseline with minimal time investment, allowing focus on skill training and homestead work.

### 5. 80/20 Analysis
**Principle**: 20% of inputs produce 80% of outputs.

**Applications**:
- Which 20% of skills provide 80% of survival capability?
- Critical gear vs. nice-to-have
- High-impact training vs. low-return activities

**Pantry Staples**:
- Dried beans (25lb bags): black, pinto, lentils, chickpeas
- Canned beans (backup)
- Canned proteins (tuna, salmon, chicken)
- Oils (olive, coconut)
- Spices

**Freezer**:
- Bulk proteins (beef, chicken, salmon)
- Frozen vegetables

**Fresh** (weekly or homestead-grown):
- Eggs (5 dozen)
- Vegetables
- Avocados

### 2. MED Strength Training (Occam's Protocol)
**Minimal Maintenance**:
- 2 workouts per week
- Workout A: Squat, Overhead Press
- Workout B: Deadlift, Bench Press
- 1 set to failure per exercise (after warm-up)
- Progressive overload tracking

### 3. Self-Experimentation Templates
**Testing Protocol**:
1. Hypothesis (clear prediction)
2. Baseline measurement (quantify current state)
3. Single variable change
4. 2-4 week testing period
5. Objective measurement
6. Analysis (keep/modify/discard)

### 4. Progress Tracking System
**Measurements** (weekly):
