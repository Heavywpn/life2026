## ‚ùÑÔ∏è NUTRITION IN EXTREME CONDITIONS

### Cold Weather Survival (Lessons from James Alofs' -22¬∞C Winter)

**Caloric Increase:**
- **-10¬∞C to 0¬∞C:** +10-20% calories
- **-10¬∞C to -20¬∞C:** +20-30% calories
- **-20¬∞C and below:** +30-40% calories

**Why:**
- Thermogenesis (body heat production) burns extra calories
- Shivering burns 400-600 calories/hour
- Cold suppresses appetite (dangerous - must force eating)

**Cold Weather Nutrition Strategies:**
1. **Increase fat intake** (helps with thermogenesis)
2. **Hot meals matter** (psychological + core temp maintenance)
3. **Frequent small meals** (prevents hunger suppression)
4. **High-calorie snacks** (pemmican, nuts, chocolate)
5. **Hot beverages** (tea, coffee, broth for morale)

**James Alofs' Tent Fire Mistake:**
He mentions the tent fire risk from his wood stove. Lesson: **Never cook in enclosed space without ventilation.** Carbon monoxide + fire risk. Cook outside or with proper venting.

## üíß HYDRATION: THE FORGOTTEN NUTRIENT

### Water Requirements

**Baseline:**
- **Sedentary:** 30ml per kg bodyweight (82kg = 2.5L)
- **Active:** 45ml per kg bodyweight (82kg = 3.7L)
- **Heavy labor + heat:** 60ml+ per kg bodyweight (82kg = 4.9L+)

**Dehydration Performance Impact:**
- **2% body weight loss:** 10-20% performance decrease
- **4% body weight loss:** 20-30% performance decrease, heat injury risk
- **6% body weight loss:** Severe performance loss, medical emergency

**Hydration Indicators:**
- ‚úÖ Urine is light yellow/clear
- ‚úÖ Urinating every 2-4 hours
- ‚ùå Dark yellow urine = dehydrated
- ‚ùå Headache, dizziness, fatigue = severely dehydrated

**Preparedness Strategy:**
- **Storage:** 3.8L/person/day minimum (11L/day safer)
- **Filtration:** Sawyer Mini, LifeStraw, Berkey for water sources
- **Purification:** Iodine tablets, chlorine dioxide, boiling (1 minute at sea level, 3 minutes above 2,000m)

## üßÇ MICRONUTRIENTS & DEFICIENCY PREVENTION

### Critical Vitamins/Minerals for Survival Scenarios

#### VITAMIN C (Scurvy Prevention)
**Need:** 75-90mg/day (200mg+ for active/stress)
**Sources:** Citrus, tomatoes, peppers, broccoli, dried fruit
**Deficiency symptoms:** Fatigue, bleeding gums, slow wound healing
**Storage:** Canned fruit, vitamin C tablets (2-year shelf life), dried rose hips

#### VITAMIN D (Immune Function, Bone Health)
**Need:** 600-800 IU/day (2000 IU+ in winter/low sun)
**Sources:** Fatty fish, egg yolks, fortified milk, sunlight
**Deficiency symptoms:** Weakened immunity, bone pain, depression
**Storage:** Vitamin D3 supplements (2-year shelf life)

#### IRON (Oxygen Transport, Energy)
**Need:** 8-18mg/day (higher for women)
**Sources:** Red meat, beans, fortified cereals, dark leafy greens
**Deficiency symptoms:** Fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath
**Storage:** Canned beef, dried beans, iron supplements

#### MAGNESIUM (Muscle Function, Sleep)
**Need:** 310-420mg/day
**Sources:** Nuts, seeds, whole grains, dark chocolate
**Deficiency symptoms:** Muscle cramps, anxiety, sleep problems
**Storage:** Nuts/seeds (vacuum-sealed), magnesium supplements

#### ELECTROLYTES (Sodium, Potassium)
**Need:** Sodium 1,500-2,300mg, Potassium 2,600-3,400mg
**Sources:** Salt, canned vegetables, beans, dried fruit
**Deficiency symptoms:** Muscle cramps, confusion, heart issues
**Storage:** Salt (infinite shelf life), electrolyte powder

**Multi-Vitamin Insurance:**
Store 1-year supply of quality multivitamin. Not a replacement for food, but insurance against deficiency during crisis.

## ü•ó THE SLOW-CARB DIET: SIMPLE NUTRITION PROTOCOL

**Source:** The 4-Hour Body by Tim Ferriss (Life-Changing Book - 5/5)
**Analysis:** `books/life-changing/the-4-hour-body-analysis.md`

### Why Slow-Carb for Preparedness?

**Alignment with Survival Project:**
- **Simple meal planning** (reduces decision fatigue during stress)
- **Repeatable protocols** (same meals = predictable food storage)
- **No calorie counting** (easier to maintain long-term)
- **Sustainable energy** (stable blood sugar for manual labor)
- **Food storage friendly** (legumes, canned proteins, frozen vegetables)

### The 5 Rules of Slow-Carb

**Rule 1: Avoid White Carbohydrates**
- No bread, rice, pasta, potatoes, grains (even "whole grain")
- If it's white or comes in white varieties, avoid it
- Exception: Cauliflower and white fish are fine

**Rule 2: Eat the Same Few Meals Repeatedly**
- Pick 3-4 meals you like
- Rotate through them for 6 days/week
- Simplicity = adherence
- Example meals below

**Rule 3: Don't Drink Calories**
- Water, unsweetened tea, black coffee only
- Exception: Dry red wine (max 2 glasses/day)
- No fruit juice, soda, milk, sports drinks

**Rule 4: Don't Eat Fruit**
- Fruit contains fructose (converts to fat easily)
- Exception: Tomatoes and avocados (technically fruits, but allowed)
- Save fruit for cheat day

**Rule 5: Take One Day Off Per Week (Cheat Day)**
- Saturday = eat whatever you want
- Prevents metabolic slowdown
- Psychological sustainability
- Go crazy (helps long-term adherence)

**Don't Use Scale Weight (Misleading)**

## üß™ PERFORMANCE NUTRITION: TIMING & OPTIMIZATION

### Pre-Workout/Labor Nutrition

**Goal:** Fuel the work without digestive distress

**Timing:**
- **2-3 hours before:** Full meal (protein + carbs + fat)
- **30-60 minutes before:** Light snack (carbs + small protein)

**Example (2 hours before heavy work):**
- 2 cups oatmeal
- 2 eggs
- 1 banana
- Water

**Example (30 min before):**
- Apple with peanut butter
- Energy bar
- Water

---

### During Work Nutrition

**For work lasting 60-90+ minutes:**
- Consume 30-60g carbs per hour
- Drink 8-16oz water every 15-20 minutes
- Electrolytes if sweating heavily (>1 hour intense work)

**Practical options:**
- Trail mix
- Dried fruit
- Energy bars
- Honey (directly from container)
- Jerky (protein + electrolytes from salt)

---

### Post-Workout/Labor Nutrition (Recovery Window)

**Goal:** Replenish glycogen, repair muscle damage

**Timing:**
- **Within 30-60 minutes:** Fast-digesting protein + carbs (3:1 or 4:1 ratio)
- **Within 2 hours:** Full meal with protein + carbs + fat

**Example (30 min post-work):**
- Protein shake with banana and honey (40g protein, 60g carbs)

**Example (2 hours post-work):**
- 8oz chicken breast
- 2 cups rice
- Vegetables with olive oil
- Water

**Why It Matters:**
This is when your body is most efficient at rebuilding muscle and restoring energy. Miss this window repeatedly, and recovery suffers.

## üö´ COMMON NUTRITION MISTAKES IN SURVIVAL SCENARIOS

### 1. Under-Eating (The Silent Killer)

**The Mistake:**
Rationing food too aggressively in a crisis, leading to performance decline.

**Why It Happens:**
- Fear of running out of food
- Stress suppresses appetite (especially in cold)
- Underestimating caloric needs of manual labor

**The Fix:**
- Calculate actual caloric needs (BMR √ó activity multiplier)
- Force eating on a schedule even if not hungry
- Prioritize performance over conservation (weak = more mistakes = worse outcomes)

**Example:**
James Alofs building his cabin needed ~4,500 cal/day. If he rationed down to 2,000 cal/day, he'd lose strength, make mistakes (like his wall stacking error), and slow progress drastically.

---

### 2. Ignoring Protein in Long-Term Situations

**The Mistake:**
Stockpiling carbs (rice, pasta) but neglecting protein sources.

**Why It Happens:**
- Protein is more expensive
- Carbs are easier to store long-term
- Don't feel the impact immediately

**The Fix:**
- Minimum 1.3g protein per kg bodyweight even in crisis
- Store canned meats, dried beans, protein powder
- Hunt/fish if possible for fresh protein

**Consequence:**
- Muscle wasting (body cannibalizes muscle for amino acids)
- Weakened immune system
- Slow wound healing
- Death spiral in long-term survival

---

### 3. Dehydration (The Performance Destroyer)

**The Mistake:**
Not drinking enough water during physical work.

**Why It Happens:**
- Cold weather suppresses thirst
- Busy with tasks, forget to drink
- Rationing water out of fear

**The Fix:**
- Schedule hydration (every 20-30 minutes during work)
- Monitor urine color
- Drink before thirsty (thirst = already mildly dehydrated)

**Consequence:**
- 2% dehydration = 10-20% performance loss
- Cognitive decline (bad decisions)
- Heat injury in warm weather
- Hypothermia risk in cold (dehydration impairs thermogenesis)

---

### 4. Neglecting Fats in Cold Weather

**The Mistake:**
Low-fat diet when operating in cold environments.

**Why It Happens:**
- Modern dietary advice emphasizes low-fat
- Carbs are cheaper and easier to store
- Don't understand thermogenesis

**The Fix:**
- Increase fat to 35-40% of calories in cold weather
- Pemmican, nuts, coconut oil, fatty meat
- Hot fatty broths (bone broth with fat)

**Consequence:**
- Impaired thermogenesis (can't stay warm)
- Hormone disruption
- Vitamin deficiency (A, D, E, K are fat-soluble)

---

### 5. Single Food Source Dependence

**The Mistake:**
Stockpiling only rice, or only beans, or only MREs.

**Why It Happens:**
- Simplicity
- Cost
- Storage space constraints

**The Fix:**
- Diversity = nutritional completeness + psychological resilience
- Minimum 3 protein sources, 3 carb sources, 2 fat sources
- Include spices and comfort foods (morale matters)

**Consequence:**
- Micronutrient deficiencies
- Food fatigue (mental breakdown from eating same thing daily)
- Incomplete amino acid profiles (if relying only on single plant protein)
