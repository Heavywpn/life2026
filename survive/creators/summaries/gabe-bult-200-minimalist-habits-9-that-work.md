## The 9 Habits That Actually Work

### 1. Minimalist Morning & Evening Routine

**Core Principle:** Short, simple routines that you can fall into consistently.

**Morning Routine:**
- First thing: Large glass of water (not screen)
- Same things, same order, every day
- Same clothes (decision fatigue reduction)
- Exact same coffee preparation
- Maximum consistency = maximum mindfulness

**Evening Routine:**
- Lay out water and clothes for next day
- Read a book instead of phone
- Set up for success

**Key Quote:**
> "Having that purposefulness and stability first thing in the morning really helps me be mindful the rest of the day and make sure that I'm doing things because I know this will help me and not just because I had a phone and picked it up and started scrolling."

**Why It Works:** Anchor habits create momentum. Not hour-long rituals - just a few consistent actions.

---

### 2. The Boring Approach to Money

**Core Principle:** Keep finances extremely simple. Invest in what you understand.

**Implementation:**
- **Ignore what you don't understand** (don't try to do it)
- **Learn OR ignore** - never act without understanding
- **Index funds > Active management** (S&P 500 beats actively managed funds 90% of time)

**Gabe's Investment Hierarchy:**
1. **YouTube** - Invest in own channels, ROI within ~1 year
2. **Real Estate** - 8 years experience, understands local market
3. **Index Funds/ETFs** - Simple, low-cost, long-term

**Mistakes Made:**
- Lost thousands on meme coins and individual stocks from "tips"
- Tried trading without research

**Key Quote:**
> "You don't need to pay somebody a percentage to do worse for you. You can just do the simplest thing."

**Book Reference:** "The Simple Path to Wealth" - just invest in low-cost index funds, set and forget.

---

### 3. The 77% Rule

**Core Principle:** How you spend your free time determines your wealth and success.

**The Data:**
- Average American: 6-7 hours/day passively consuming content
- **67% of wealthy people:** Watch less than 1 hour TV/day
- **77% of poor people:** Watch more than 1 hour TV/day

**Gabe's Application:**
- No new TV shows started in 3-4 years
- Switched from consuming every night to building with his "5-to-9"
- Result: Left his job, built income streams

**The Trap:**
```
Work really hard → Have to recover → Sleep → Repeat
```

This loop never ends if you don't convert recovery time into productive time.

**Key Quote:**
> "If your house is a wreck, you're out of shape, you don't have enough money, I would recommend that the first thing you change is how are you spending your nights and your free time. Are you mindlessly consuming content? Or are you bettering yourself?"

**The Escape:**
- Stop "unwinding" and "treating yourself" every night
- Use evening hours to build things that create freedom
- Replace consumption with productive activities that are ALSO enjoyable

---

### 4. Free Zones (Two Types)

#### Type 1: Phone-Free Zones

**Designated spaces where phone cannot go:**
- Sauna
- Cold plunge
- Bed

**Purpose:** Clarity of mind. Places to think, process, reset. No music, no input.

#### Type 2: Clutter-Free Zones

**Spaces that MUST remain clutter-free:**
- Office/workspace
- Kitchen counters
- Entry points (front door)

**Implementation:**
- 5-minute cleanup at end of every night
- Reset these zones for next day
- Covered counters = stress walking in the door

**Key Quote:**
> "If that part of your house stays clean and you really, really focus on it, it'll impact the rest of your house. It'll impact your stress levels."

---

### 5. Always Be Learning (Control Your Inputs)

**Core Principle:** Your inputs control your thoughts. You can't have thoughts about things you don't know exist.

**Application:**
- Want to get in shape? → Flood brain with fitness content
- Want to improve finances? → Consume finance content
- Struggling in any area? → Deliberately choose inputs for that area

**Key Quote:**
> "Pretty much your inputs are going to control what you think. You can't have thoughts if you don't know that those things exist."

**Warning:** Be very cautious and choose inputs deliberately. Garbage in = garbage out.

---

### 6. 5-Minute Cleanup (Nightly Reset)

**Implementation:**
- Every night, spend 5 minutes resetting key spaces
- Office: Clear desk, prepare for next day
- Kitchen: Clear counters, fill dishwasher
- Sets up next day for success

**Why It Works:**
- Prevents accumulation
- Reduces morning friction
- Creates psychological fresh start daily
