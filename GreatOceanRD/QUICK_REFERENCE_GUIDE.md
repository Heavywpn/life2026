### What to Pack for Weather

**Essential:**
- SPF 50+ sunscreen (multiple bottles!)
- Wide-brim hats
- Sunglasses
- Light layers (mornings/evenings cooler on coast)
- Swimmers
- Rain jacket (Melbourne is unpredictable)

**Heat Management:**
- Extra water (3-5L per person per day)
- Cooling towels
- Portable fan for tent
- Seek shade midday

### Fire Danger Season

**December = Peak fire season in southern Australia**

**Daily Actions:**
- Check fire danger rating each morning
- Download: "Fires Near Me" apps (VIC & SA)
- Listen to ABC Local Radio for updates
- NO campfires on Total Fire Ban days
- Gas stoves usually OK (check restrictions)
- Have evacuation plan if camping in bushland

**Total Fire Ban Days:**
- No campfires
- Some areas: no BBQs (electric might be OK)
- Be extremely careful
- Change plans if "Code Red" fire danger

## Pro Tips

### Great Ocean Road Specific

1. **Start early** - Beat crowds at Twelve Apostles (arrive before 9am)
2. **Drive slow** - It's about the journey, not the destination
3. **Stop often** - Every lookout has different view
4. **Look for koalas** - Kennett River and Cape Otway are best
5. **Respect the ocean** - Dangerous currents, never turn back on waves
6. **Book accommodation early** - Can't emphasize this enough!
7. **Fuel up regularly** - Stations are spread out
8. **Check fire danger** - Daily in December

### Camping Tips

1. **Arrive by 3pm** - Set up camp in daylight
2. **Secure your site** - Peg everything (coastal winds!)
3. **Keep food sealed** - Ants and wildlife everywhere
4. **Respect quiet hours** - Usually 10pm-7am
5. **Use camp kitchens** - Save gas, meet other travelers
6. **Try campground BBQs** - Usually free, well-maintained
7. **Be a good neighbor** - Respect other campers' space

### Money Saving

1. **Cook at camp** - Saves $50+ per day
2. **Free attractions** - Most GOR viewing is free
3. **BYO alcohol** - Cheaper than buying in towns
4. **Fuel strategy** - Fill in major towns
5. **Pack snacks** - Tourist areas overcharge
6. **Camp rather than hotel** - Save $100+ per night
7. **Free camping** - Use WikiCamps (but harder in Dec)

## What If Things Go Wrong?

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Camping fully booked:**
- Check Hipcamp for private land
- Call campgrounds for cancellations
- Stay in hotels/motels (have backup budget)
- Be flexible with locations
- Free camping (WikiCamps app)

**Vehicle breakdown:**
- Call roadside assistance immediately
- Have membership card handy
- Don't attempt repairs on busy highways
- Have contingency time in schedule

**Bad weather:**
- Melbourne/Adelaide have museums, indoor attractions
- Adjust outdoor activities to better days
- Coastal areas can be windy - drive carefully
- Rain makes roads slippery

**Fire danger/Total Fire Ban:**
- Use gas stove (usually allowed)
- No campfires
- Consider staying in towns with facilities
- Monitor ABC Local Radio
- Have evacuation route planned

**Illness/injury:**
- Use first aid kit for minor issues
- Find nearest hospital (Google Maps)
- Call 000 for emergencies
- Travel insurance may cover medical

## Emergency Evacuation Plan

**In case of bushfire or severe weather:**

1. **Monitor warnings:** ABC Local Radio, Emergency apps
2. **Leave early:** Don't wait for official evacuation
3. **Head to:** Nearest major town or beach area
4. **Have:** Full tank of fuel, water, phones charged
5. **Know your route:** Multiple escape routes from camp
6. **Stay informed:** Keep radio on, check apps regularly

**Safer Place Options on Route:**
- Major towns with evacuation centers
- Beaches (if fire threatens)
- Stay in vehicle on cleared road (if trapped)

**DON'T:**
- Ignore warnings
- Think you can defend campsite
- Drive through smoke (zero visibility)
- Separate from partner (stay together!)
