### Day 6: December 28 - Apollo Bay ‚Üí Port Campbell
**Distance:** ~100 km (2 hours driving, but FULL DAY with stops)

**THIS IS THE HIGHLIGHT DAY!**

**Morning:**
- **Cape Otway Lightstation** (30 min detour from main road)
  - Australia's oldest surviving lighthouse
  - Spectacular coastal views
  - Often see koalas in surrounding trees
  - Entry fee applies

- **Bimbi Park Camping Area** (near Cape Otway)
  - Unique camping among koalas (if you camp tonight instead)

**Midday/Afternoon - THE BIG ATTRACTIONS:**

**Route becomes spectacular after Princetown**

1. **Gibson Steps**
   - First close-up view of limestone stacks
   - Walk down to beach level (if not rough seas)
   - 15-20 minutes

2. **THE TWELVE APOSTLES** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
   - THE most iconic attraction
   - Multiple viewing platforms
   - Visit at sunrise/sunset for best light (if camping nearby)
   - Can be extremely crowded 10am-4pm
   - Allow 30-45 minutes

3. **Loch Ard Gorge** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
   - Stunning gorge with tragic shipwreck history
   - Multiple viewing platforms and beach access
   - Don't miss the Island Archway nearby
   - Allow 45 minutes

4. **The Grotto**
   - Beautiful rock formation and pool
   - Short walk from car park
   - 15-20 minutes

5. **London Arch** (formerly London Bridge)
   - Natural arch formation
   - Great photo opportunity
   - 10-15 minutes

6. **The Arch and Mutton Bird Island**
   - More stunning coastal formations
   - Often less crowded

**Evening - Port Campbell:**
- Small town, main base for Twelve Apostles area
- Sunset viewing from Port Campbell Beach

**Camping Options:**
- **Port Campbell Recreation Reserve** (near the beach)
- **Port Campbell National Park Camping**

**CRITICAL:** Book well ahead - this is the most popular camping area on Great Ocean Road

**Tips:**
- Start EARLY (sunrise at Twelve Apostles is magical and crowd-free)
- Fill up fuel in Apollo Bay
- Next fuel: Port Campbell or Warrnambool
- Bring food/snacks - limited options between towns
- Download offline maps
