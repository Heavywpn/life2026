# BNI Chapter Presentations

## Audience Profile

### Who They Are
- Local business owners and professionals
- Seeking qualified referrals
- Build relationships through regular meetings
- Value trust and reliability
- Mix of industries

### What They Care About
- Quality referrals they can trust
- Understanding exactly what you do
- Knowing who to refer to you
- Building genuine relationships
- Seeing commitment to the group

### What They Don't Want
- Generic corporate presentations
- Hard sells
- Unclear service offerings
- Someone who won't reciprocate referrals

---

## BNI-Specific Considerations

### Time Limits
- Weekly 60-second commercials
- 10-minute feature presentations
- Stick to time religiously

### Tone
- Professional but personal
- Authentic stories over sales pitch
- Educational value
- Show your expertise without jargon

### Goals
1. Make it easy for members to refer you
2. Be memorable
3. Build trust and credibility
4. Show your unique value
5. Demonstrate commitment to reciprocity

---

## What Works in BNI

### Effective Approaches
- Personal stories with business connection
- Clear examples of ideal clients
- Specific referral requests
- Demonstrating your values
- Showing you understand business pain points

### What to Avoid
- Technical jargon without explanation
- Boring PowerPoints
- Reading from slides
- Generic "trust me" statements
- Forgetting to give clear referral guidance

## Feature Presentation Structure

### Opening (1-2 min)
- Hook them immediately
- Your stereotype-breaking visual sequence
- "Belt-fed machine gun" line

### Middle (6-7 min)
- One powerful military story (East Timor)
- Connect to business principles
- Show unfairness in IT industry
- How you fight it differently
- Specific examples/case studies

### Close (1-2 min)
- Callback to opening
- Clear referral request
- What to look for
- How to introduce you
- Memorable final line

## Weekly 60-Second Formats

### Format 1: Problem/Solution
"This week I met a business owner who [problem]. Here's what we did [solution]. If you know someone dealing with [similar problem], I can help."

### Format 2: Referral Focus
"My ideal client this week is [specific description]. They're usually dealing with [pain point]. Best introduction is [method]."

### Format 3: Education
"Quick tip this week: [valuable insight]. This is the kind of proactive approach we bring to all our clients."

### Format 4: Story
"10 years ago in East Timor, [brief story]. Today in business, [same principle]. Looking for partners who value [value]."
