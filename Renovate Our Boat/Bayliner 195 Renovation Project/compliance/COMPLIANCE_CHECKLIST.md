# Australian & Queensland Boat Compliance Checklist

## Overview
This checklist ensures the Bayliner 195 meets all Australian and Queensland maritime safety and registration requirements.

---

## 1. Queensland Maritime Registration

### Registration Requirements
- [ ] Valid Queensland boat registration
- [ ] Registration label displayed on both sides of hull
- [ ] Registration renewal up to date
- [ ] Ownership documentation current
- [ ] Transfer of registration (if applicable)

### Identification
- [ ] Hull Identification Number (HIN) visible and legible
- [ ] Registration number displayed correctly (150mm high, contrasting color)
- [ ] Labels positioned correctly (forward half of vessel, both sides)

---

## 2. Safety Equipment (USL 1D - Smooth/Partially Smooth Waters)

### Personal Flotation Devices (PFDs)
- [ ] Australian Standard PFD Type 1, 2, or 3 for each person on board
- [ ] PFDs in serviceable condition
- [ ] Appropriate sizes available
- [ ] Easily accessible storage

### Fire Fighting Equipment
- [ ] Minimum 1 x 1kg dry powder fire extinguisher (AS/NZS 1841)
- [ ] Fire extinguisher serviced and in date
- [ ] Fire extinguisher readily accessible
- [ ] Mounted securely

### Visual Distress Signals
- [ ] 3 red hand flares (in-date)
- [ ] OR 1 orange hand smoke signal
- [ ] Stored in watertight container
- [ ] Check expiry dates regularly

### Anchoring Equipment
- [ ] Anchor suitable for vessel size
- [ ] Minimum 30m of anchor line
- [ ] Anchor line appropriate diameter for vessel
- [ ] Chain component (recommended)

### Bilge Pump
- [ ] Manual or electric bilge pump operational
- [ ] Bilge clean and free of oil/fuel
- [ ] Bilge pump tested and functioning

### Sound Signaling
- [ ] Whistle, horn, or similar device
- [ ] Audible for at least 0.5 nautical miles
- [ ] Readily accessible

### Additional Safety Items (Recommended)
- [ ] Bailer or bucket
- [ ] Paddle or oars
- [ ] First aid kit
- [ ] Torch (waterproof)
- [ ] VHF radio or EPIRB
- [ ] GPS/navigation equipment
- [ ] Navigation lights (if operating at night)

---

## 3. Navigation Lights (If Operating Between Sunset & Sunrise)

- [ ] Masthead white light (all-round)
- [ ] Red port light (left side)
- [ ] Green starboard light (right side)
- [ ] White stern light
- [ ] All lights functioning correctly
- [ ] Lights meet visibility range requirements

---

## 4. Engine & Fuel System Compliance

### Engine Requirements
- [ ] Engine in good working order
- [ ] No fuel leaks
- [ ] Fuel lines in good condition
- [ ] Proper ventilation for engine compartment
- [ ] Engine kill switch operational
- [ ] Cooling system functional

### Fuel System
- [ ] Fuel tank properly secured
- [ ] Fuel cap seals properly
- [ ] No deterioration of fuel lines
- [ ] Fuel/water separator (if fitted) serviced
- [ ] Portable fuel tanks meet AS 2907

---

## 5. Environmental Compliance

- [ ] No discharge of sewage in restricted areas
- [ ] Holding tank (if fitted) operational
- [ ] No oil/fuel discharge
- [ ] Rubbish disposal plan (take all waste ashore)
- [ ] Bilge oil-free

---

## 6. Structural & Hull Integrity

- [ ] Hull free from significant damage
- [ ] No soft spots or delamination
- [ ] Transom sound and secure
- [ ] Through-hull fittings secure (no leaks)
- [ ] Drain plug present and operational
- [ ] Hatches and covers secure

---

## 7. Operator Requirements

### Licensing
- [ ] Vessel operator holds valid Recreational Marine Driver Licence (boat license)
- [ ] License covers appropriate vessel category
- [ ] Understand local waterway rules
- [ ] Aware of speed limits and restrictions

### Insurance (Recommended)
- [ ] Comprehensive boat insurance
- [ ] Third-party liability coverage
- [ ] Trailer insurance

---

## 8. Marine Parks & Restricted Areas

- [ ] Understand Marine Park zones in area of operation
- [ ] Aware of restricted/prohibited areas
- [ ] Know speed limits in designated areas
- [ ] Understand anchoring restrictions

---

## 9. Pre-Departure Checklist

- [ ] Weather forecast checked
- [ ] Float plan filed (notify someone of plans)
- [ ] All safety equipment aboard and accessible
- [ ] Fuel sufficient for trip + reserve
- [ ] Battery charged
- [ ] Bilge checked and dry
- [ ] All passengers briefed on safety
