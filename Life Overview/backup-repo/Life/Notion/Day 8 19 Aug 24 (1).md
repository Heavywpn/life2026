  

## Summary

The note covers three main sections of scripture from the Old Testament, New Testament, and Proverbs:

### Old Testament: Genesis 17:1 - 18:33

- **The Covenant of Circumcision:** God changes Abram's name to Abraham and institutes circumcision as a sign of the covenant.
- **The Three Visitors:** Three visitors promise Sarah a son, emphasizing God's power.
- **Abraham Pleads for Sodom:** Abraham intercedes for Sodom, showcasing his relationship with God and concern for others.

### New Testament: Matthew 6:25 - 7:23

- **Do Not Worry:** Jesus teaches trust in God's provision.
- **Judging Others:** Instructions against hypocritical judgment.
- **Ask, Seek, Knock:** Encouragement for persistent prayer and the Golden Rule.
- **The Narrow and Wide Gates:** Contrast between the paths to life and destruction.
- **True and False Prophets:** Warning against false prophets.
- **True Disciples:** Emphasis on doing God's will.

### Proverbs 1:8-19

- **Parental Instruction:** Importance of listening to parental wisdom.
- **Warning Against Evil Companions:** Resist peer pressure and avoid sinful paths.
- **Consequences of Wickedness:** Self-destructive nature of greed and evil.

## Reflection on Leadership

From these scriptures, several leadership lessons can be drawn:

1. **Faith and Obedience:** Just as Abraham demonstrated faith and obedience to God, a good leader should cultivate trust and adherence to core values and principles.
2. **Hospitality and Inclusion:** Abraham's hospitality to the visitors teaches leaders the importance of welcoming and valuing diverse perspectives.
3. **Intercession and Advocacy:** Abraham's intercession for Sodom reflects a leader's role in advocating for and protecting the well-being of their community.
4. **Trust in Provision:** Jesus' teaching on not worrying about basic needs highlights the importance of trusting in the support systems and resources available.
5. **Judgment and Self-Reflection:** Leaders should evaluate their own actions and motives before passing judgment on others.
6. **Persistent Effort:** The lesson on asking, seeking, and knocking underscores the value of perseverance and continuous effort in leadership.
7. **Discernment:** Recognizing true and false prophets teaches leaders to discern genuine talent and integrity in their team.
8. **Genuine Action:** Emphasizing true discipleship over mere profession of faith encourages leaders to act authentically and in alignment with their stated values.
9. **Wisdom and Guidance:** Proverbs' emphasis on listening to wise counsel and avoiding destructive behaviors highlights the importance of seeking and providing sound guidance.

By incorporating these principles, leaders can develop a more compassionate, discerning, and effective leadership style.

# Old testament

Genesis 17:1 - 18:33: The Covenant of Circumcision

In this passage, God reaffirms His covenant with Abram, changing his name to Abraham and promising him numerous descendants. God institutes circumcision as a sign of this covenant.

Key points:

- God changes Abram's name to Abraham, meaning "father of many nations"
- Sarah's name is changed from Sarai, and she is promised a son
- Circumcision is established as a covenant sign for all male descendants
- God promises to bless Ishmael, but establishes His covenant through Isaac
- Abraham intercedes for Sodom and Gomorrah

This passage highlights the importance of faith, obedience, and God's faithfulness to His promises.

- The Three Visitors (Genesis 18:1-15)
    
    In this section, three visitors appear to Abraham near the great trees of Mamre.
    
    - Abraham welcomes the visitors and offers them hospitality
    - The visitors promise that Sarah will have a son within a year
    - Sarah, overhearing this, laughs in disbelief due to her old age
    - The Lord questions why Sarah laughed, emphasizing that nothing is too hard for Him
    
    This passage highlights themes of hospitality, faith, and God's miraculous power to fulfill His promises.
    
    - Abraham Pleads for Sodom (Genesis 18:16-33)
        
        After the promise of Isaac's birth, the Lord decides to reveal His plans for Sodom and Gomorrah to Abraham.
        
        - Abraham intercedes for the righteous in Sodom, negotiating with God
        - He asks if God would spare the city for 50 righteous people, gradually reducing the number to 10
        - God agrees to spare the city if 10 righteous people are found
        - This passage demonstrates Abraham's close relationship with God and his concern for others
        
        This interaction highlights themes of divine justice, mercy, and the power of intercessory prayer. It also shows Abraham's growth in faith and his boldness in approaching God.
        

# New Testament

# Matthew 6:25 - 7:23: Teachings on Trust, Judgment, and True Discipleship

This passage contains some of Jesus' most profound teachings from the Sermon on the Mount. It covers various aspects of Christian living, including trust in God, proper attitudes, and the path of true discipleship.

- Do Not Worry (Matthew 6:25-34)
    - Jesus teaches not to worry about basic needs like food and clothing
    - He emphasizes trusting in God's provision, as He cares for all creation
    - Encourages seeking God's kingdom and righteousness first
- Judging Others (Matthew 7:1-6)
    - Jesus warns against judging others hypocritically
    - Instructs to examine one's own faults before criticizing others
    - Cautions against giving what is holy to those who won't appreciate it
- Ask, Seek, Knock (Matthew 7:7-12)
    - Encourages persistent prayer and trust in God's goodness
    - Compares God's generosity to that of earthly fathers
    - Introduces the Golden Rule: treat others as you want to be treated
- The Narrow and Wide Gates (Matthew 7:13-14)
    - Contrasts the narrow gate leading to life with the wide gate leading to destruction
    - Emphasizes that the path to life is challenging and found by few
- True and False Prophets (Matthew 7:15-20)
    - Warns against false prophets who appear harmless but are inwardly destructive
    - Teaches to recognize people by the fruits of their actions
- True Disciples (Matthew 7:21-23)
    - Stresses that not everyone who claims to follow Jesus is truly His disciple
    - Emphasizes the importance of doing God's will, not just professing faith

This passage highlights themes of faith, trust in God, discernment, and the importance of genuine discipleship. It challenges believers to examine their hearts and actions in light of Jesus' teachings.

  

# Proverbs

- Proverbs 1:8-19: Warnings Against Evil Companions
    
    This passage offers wisdom from a father to his son, emphasizing the importance of heeding parental instruction and avoiding the temptations of sinful companions.
    
    - Verses 8-9: Importance of parental instruction
        - Listen to your father's instruction
        - Do not forsake your mother's teaching
        - They are a garland and a chain, adorning you
    - Verses 10-14: Warning against enticement by sinners
        - Resist peer pressure to join in evil deeds
        - Description of the temptations offered by sinners
    - Verses 15-19: Consequences of choosing the wrong path
        - Avoid the path of evildoers
        - Those who pursue evil bring harm upon themselves
        - Greed leads to the destruction of the greedy
    
    Key themes in this passage include the value of wisdom, the importance of resisting temptation, and the self-destructive nature of wickedness. It serves as a foundation for the practical wisdom that follows in the book of Proverbs.
