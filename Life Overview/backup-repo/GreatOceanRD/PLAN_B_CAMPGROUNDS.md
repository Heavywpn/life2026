## GENERAL BACKUP STRATEGIES

### If You Can't Find Camping:

**1. Hipcamp (Private Land Camping)**
- Download Hipcamp app
- Search along your route
- Private properties offer camping
- Often available when traditional campgrounds full
- Usually $20-40/night
- Variable facilities (read reviews)

**2. Free Camping (WikiCamps)**
- Download WikiCamps Australia
- Filter: Free camping
- **Reality Check:** Free camps Dec 25-Jan 2 = packed, unreliable
- Better as emergency backup than plan
- Most are "first come first served" = arrive early (by noon)

**3. Roadside Rest Areas (Emergency Only)**
- Many allow overnight camping (check signs)
- NO facilities (maybe toilet)
- Use WikiCamps to find ones allowing camping
- **Only use if absolutely stuck**

**4. Hotel/Motel Last Minute**
- Download booking.com app
- "Tonight" deals sometimes available
- Budget: $100-180/night
- Most towns on route have motels

**5. Adjust Route**
- If critical area (Port Campbell, Robe) fully booked
- Consider staying extra day elsewhere
- Example: Skip Port Fairy, stay Warrnambool 2 nights
- Flexibility = key to success

"Hi, I'm hoping to book camping for [date(s)].
- **Dates:** [Check-in date] to [Check-out date]
- **Number of nights:** [X]
- **Vehicle:** Toyota Hilux with camping equipment
- **People:** 2 adults
- **Site needed:** Powered site preferred

If you're fully booked, could you recommend nearby alternatives that might have availability?

Can you also tell me:
- Site size (will Hilux fit?)
- Check-in/check-out times?
- Total cost?
- Cancellation policy?

Thank you!"

**If they say fully booked:**
- Ask about cancellation waitlist
- Ask for recommendations of nearby campgrounds
- Thank them and immediately call next option
