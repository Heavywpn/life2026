### Day 5: January 5 - MOUNT KOSCIUSZKO SUMMIT WALK! üèîÔ∏è

**AUSTRALIA'S HIGHEST PEAK - 2,228 meters**

**This is the highlight of your return journey!**

### Walk Options:

**Option 1: Charlotte Pass Route** (RECOMMENDED for Summer)

**Details:**
- **Start:** Charlotte Pass (1,760m elevation)
- **End:** Mount Kosciuszko Summit (2,228m)
- **Distance:** 13km return (6.5km each way)
- **Time:** 5-6 hours return (including summit time)
- **Elevation Gain:** 468m (easier than Thredbo)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate - good fitness required but achievable
- **Path:** Well-maintained track, mostly above treeline

**Why Charlotte Pass:**
- Shorter walk to summit (2.5-3 hours up)
- Less elevation gain
- Beautiful alpine scenery throughout
- Less crowded than Thredbo route
- Stunning views entire way

**Getting There:**
- Jindabyne ‚Üí Charlotte Pass: 45 mins drive
- Road is sealed, Hilux will handle fine
- Park at Charlotte Pass car park (fee included in park entry)

**Option 2: Thredbo Route via Chairlift**

**Details:**
- **Start:** Thredbo Village ‚Üí Chairlift to Eagles Nest (2,037m)
- **Walk Start:** Eagles Nest
- **Distance:** 13km return from chairlift top
- **Time:** 5-6 hours return
- **Elevation Gain:** ~200m from chairlift top
- **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate

**Costs:**
- Chairlift: ~$49 return per person = $98 total
- Park entry: ~$17

**Advantage:**
- Chairlift removes hardest elevation gain
- More accessible for all fitness levels
- Iconic Thredbo experience

**Disadvantage:**
- More expensive
- More crowded (most popular route)
- Chairlift operating hours limit your time

### What to Bring on the Walk:

**Essential:**
- [ ] 2-3 liters water each (refill at Charlotte Pass before starting)
- [ ] Lunch and snacks (sandwiches, fruit, nuts, energy bars)
- [ ] Sunscreen SPF50+ (alpine sun is INTENSE)
- [ ] Sunglasses (essential - snow glare even in summer)
- [ ] Wide-brim hat
- [ ] Comfortable hiking boots or shoes (worn in, not new!)
- [ ] Layered clothing (starts cool, warms up, cools at top)

**Clothing Layers:**
- Base layer: Moisture-wicking shirt
- Mid layer: Light fleece or long-sleeve
- Outer layer: Windproof jacket (summit can be windy)
- Pants: Hiking pants or comfortable athletic pants
- Extra layer in pack (weather changes fast in mountains)

**Additional Items:**
- [ ] Backpack (25-30L)
- [ ] First aid kit (basic)
- [ ] Phone (download offline map, check cell coverage poor)
- [ ] Camera + extra battery (you'll take lots of photos!)
- [ ] Walking poles (optional but helpful)
- [ ] Toilet paper + trowel (no facilities on trail)
- [ ] Rubbish bag (pack out all trash)

**NOT Needed:**
- Crampons/snow gear (summer = no snow required)
- Camping gear (day walk only)
- Excessive clothing (summer = warm, but bring windproof)

### Weather & Safety:

**January Conditions:**
- Temperature: 15-25¬∞C at lower elevations
- Summit: Can be 5-15¬∞C with wind chill
- Generally good weather (less rain than winter)
- Afternoon thunderstorms possible

**Safety Rules:**
1. **Start early** (8am) to avoid afternoon storms
2. **Turn back if weather deteriorates** (lightning, heavy rain)
3. **Stay on marked track** (alpine environment fragile)
4. **Carry extra water** (dehydration at altitude)
5. **Tell someone your plan** (campground staff, text friend)
6. **Emergency:** Call 000, give location (Charlotte Pass track)

**If Weather is Bad:**
- Rain/storms: Postpone walk, stay another night Jindabyne
- You have flexibility in return journey
- Don't risk it - mountain weather can be dangerous
