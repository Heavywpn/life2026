## 2. SERVICES

### 2.1 Provision of Services
The Provider agrees to provide the Services described in the Service Schedule during the Term, subject to the terms of this Agreement.

### 2.2 Service Schedule
The specific services, service levels, exclusions, and pricing are set out in the Service Schedule attached hereto. The Service Schedule forms an integral part of this Agreement.

### 2.3 Changes to Services
Either party may request changes to the Services by written notice. Changes will only be effective when agreed in writing by both parties and may result in adjustment to Fees.

### 2.4 Subcontractors
The Provider may engage subcontractors to perform some or all of the Services, provided that:
(a) The Provider remains responsible for the performance of the Services;
(b) Subcontractors are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement;
(c) For Services involving Personal Information, subcontractors comply with Privacy Act requirements.

## 3. CLIENT OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Cooperation
The Client agrees to:
(a) Provide the Provider with reasonable access to Client premises, systems, and personnel as required to perform the Services;
(b) Designate a primary contact person with authority to make decisions regarding the Services;
(c) Provide accurate and complete information as reasonably requested;
(d) Maintain appropriate software licenses for Client systems;
(e) Ensure end users comply with acceptable use policies.

### 3.2 Client Systems
The Client is responsible for:
(a) Hardware and software not expressly covered under the Services;
(b) Internet connectivity and bandwidth at Client premises;
(c) Physical security of Client premises and equipment;
(d) Backup of data on systems not covered by the Services.

### 3.3 Minimum Standards
The Provider may require the Client to maintain minimum hardware, software, and security standards. If Client systems do not meet these standards, the Provider may:
(a) Recommend remediation;
(b) Exclude non-compliant systems from the Services;
(c) Adjust Fees to reflect additional effort required.

## 4. FEES AND PAYMENT

### 4.1 Fees
The Client agrees to pay the Fees set out in the Service Schedule. Unless otherwise specified:
(a) Recurring Fees are invoiced monthly in advance;
(b) Project and ad-hoc Fees are invoiced upon completion or as specified in the SOW.

### 4.2 Payment Terms
Payment is due within 14 days of invoice date. All amounts are in Australian dollars and exclusive of GST unless stated otherwise.

### 4.3 GST
All Fees are exclusive of GST. The Client agrees to pay GST on taxable supplies in addition to the Fees.

### 4.4 Late Payment
If payment is not received by the due date:
(a) The Provider may charge interest at the rate of 2% per annum above the Reserve Bank of Australia cash rate, calculated daily;
(b) The Provider may suspend Services upon 7 days' written notice;
(c) The Client is liable for reasonable costs of debt recovery.

### 4.5 Fee Adjustments
The Provider may adjust Fees:
(a) Annually, with at least 30 days' notice, by an amount not exceeding the greater of 5% or the annual increase in the Consumer Price Index (All Groups, Brisbane);
(b) Upon material change in scope, client environment, or vendor/supplier costs, with reasonable notice and explanation.

### 4.6 Disputed Invoices
If the Client disputes any invoice in good faith, the Client must:
(a) Pay the undisputed portion by the due date;
(b) Notify the Provider in writing within 14 days of invoice date;
(c) Provide reasonable detail of the dispute.
The parties will work in good faith to resolve disputes promptly.

## 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 5.1 Term
This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for the Initial Term specified in the Service Schedule, then renews automatically for successive Renewal Terms unless terminated in accordance with this clause.

### 5.2 Termination for Convenience
Either party may terminate this Agreement:
(a) At the end of the Initial Term or any Renewal Term, by providing written notice at least 90 days before the end of the then-current term;
(b) During the Initial Term, by the Client paying an early termination fee equal to 50% of the remaining Fees for the Initial Term.

### 5.3 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if:
(a) The other party commits a material breach that is not remedied within 30 days of written notice;
(b) The other party becomes insolvent, enters administration, liquidation, or ceases to carry on business;
(c) A Force Majeure Event prevents performance for more than 60 consecutive days.

### 5.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:
(a) The Client must pay all Fees for Services provided up to termination;
(b) The Provider will provide reasonable transition assistance for up to 30 days (additional fees may apply);
(c) Each party will return or destroy Confidential Information of the other party;
(d) Clauses that by their nature should survive (including confidentiality, liability, and dispute resolution) will survive termination.

### 5.5 Data Return
Upon termination, the Provider will:
(a) Make Client data available for download or export for 30 days;
(b) Securely delete Client data from Provider systems within 90 days of termination;
(c) Provide certification of deletion upon request.

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 6.1 Obligations
Each party agrees to:
(a) Keep confidential all Confidential Information of the other party;
(b) Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement;
(c) Not disclose Confidential Information except to employees, contractors, or advisers who need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations;
(d) Take reasonable measures to protect Confidential Information.

### 6.2 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that:
(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party;
(b) Was known to the receiving party before disclosure;
(c) Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information;
(d) Is disclosed by a third party without breach of confidentiality.

### 6.3 Required Disclosure
A party may disclose Confidential Information if required by law, provided it:
(a) Gives prompt notice to the other party (where legally permitted);
(b) Limits disclosure to what is legally required;
(c) Seeks confidential treatment where available.

### 6.4 Duration
Confidentiality obligations continue for 3 years after termination of this Agreement, or indefinitely for trade secrets.

## 7. DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY

### 7.1 Privacy Act Compliance
To the extent the Provider handles Personal Information in providing the Services, the Provider will:
(a) Comply with the Australian Privacy Principles;
(b) Only collect, use, and disclose Personal Information as necessary to provide the Services;
(c) Take reasonable steps to protect Personal Information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorized access;
(d) Notify the Client promptly of any request or complaint regarding Personal Information.

### 7.2 Cross-Border Disclosure
The Provider may engage offshore subcontractors for certain Services. Before disclosing Personal Information to offshore recipients, the Provider will:
(a) Take reasonable steps to ensure the recipient complies with the APPs or equivalent privacy protections;
(b) Notify the Client of the countries where Personal Information may be processed;
(c) Remain accountable for the handling of Personal Information.

### 7.3 Data Breach Notification
If the Provider becomes aware of an actual or suspected Eligible Data Breach involving Client data:
(a) The Provider will notify the Client within 24 hours of becoming aware;
(b) The Provider will cooperate with the Client in assessing the breach;
(c) The Provider will assist the Client in meeting notification obligations under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme;
(d) Each party is responsible for notifications required by law in respect of its own obligations.

### 7.4 Security Measures
The Provider maintains security measures appropriate to the sensitivity of information handled, including:
(a) Access controls and authentication;
(b) Encryption of data in transit and at rest;
(c) Regular security assessments and updates;
(d) Staff training on security and privacy obligations.

## 10. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 10.1 Liability Cap
Subject to clause 10.3, the maximum aggregate liability of either party under or in connection with this Agreement, whether in contract, tort (including negligence), statute, or otherwise, is limited to:
(a) The greater of the Fees paid or payable by the Client in the 12 months preceding the claim; or
(b) $100,000 AUD;
whichever is greater.

### 10.2 Exclusion of Consequential Loss
Subject to clause 10.3, neither party is liable for:
(a) Loss of profits, revenue, business, or anticipated savings;
(b) Loss of data or corruption of data;
(c) Loss of goodwill or reputation;
(d) Indirect, special, or consequential loss or damage;
however arising, even if advised of the possibility of such loss.

### 10.3 Exclusions from Limitations
The limitations in clauses 10.1 and 10.2 do not apply to:
(a) Liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence;
(b) Liability for fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
(c) Breach of confidentiality obligations;
(d) Infringement of Intellectual Property rights;
(e) The Client's obligation to pay Fees;
(f) Liability that cannot be limited by law.

### 10.4 Mitigation
Each party must take reasonable steps to mitigate any loss or damage for which the other party may be liable.

### 10.5 Time Limit
Neither party may bring a claim under this Agreement more than 2 years after the date on which the cause of action arose.

## 13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 13.1 Negotiation
If a dispute arises, the parties will first attempt to resolve it through good faith negotiation between their designated representatives within 14 days.

### 13.2 Mediation
If negotiation fails, either party may refer the dispute to mediation:
(a) Mediation will be conducted by a mediator agreed by the parties or, failing agreement, appointed by the Resolution Institute;
(b) Mediation will be held in Brisbane, Queensland;
(c) Each party will bear its own costs, with mediation costs shared equally;
(d) Mediation is confidential and without prejudice.

### 13.3 Litigation
If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within 30 days, either party may commence legal proceedings in the courts of Queensland, Australia.

### 13.4 Continuation of Services
Unless the dispute relates to non-payment of Fees, the Provider will continue to provide the Services during the dispute resolution process, and the Client will continue to pay undisputed Fees.

### 13.5 Urgent Relief
Nothing in this clause prevents either party from seeking urgent interlocutory relief from a court of competent jurisdiction.
