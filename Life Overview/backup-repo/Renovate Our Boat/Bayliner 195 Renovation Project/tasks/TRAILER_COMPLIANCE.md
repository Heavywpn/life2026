# Boat Trailer Compliance & Maintenance Checklist
## Queensland, Australia

---

## Overview
This checklist ensures your boat trailer meets all Queensland and Australian road safety requirements for legal and safe towing.

---

## 1. Trailer Registration

### Registration Requirements
- [ ] Trailer is registered in Queensland
- [ ] Registration label affixed and visible
- [ ] Registration current and up to date
- [ ] Rego number matches paperwork
- [ ] Registration renewal date noted

### Identification
- [ ] VIN/Compliance plate visible
- [ ] Manufacturer details legible
- [ ] Weight ratings visible (ATM, GTM)
- [ ] Registration number displayed correctly

---

## 2. Trailer Lights & Electrical

### Required Lights (AS/NZS 1906)
- [ ] Two red tail lights (rear)
- [ ] Two red stop lights (brake lights)
- [ ] Amber turn signals (left and right)
- [ ] White number plate light
- [ ] Red reflectors (rear)
- [ ] Amber reflectors (sides)

### Light Functionality
- [ ] All lights operational
- [ ] Wiring properly sealed/waterproof
- [ ] No exposed or damaged wiring
- [ ] Plug connection clean and corrosion-free
- [ ] Lights sync with tow vehicle
- [ ] Check earth connection

### Trailer Plug
- [ ] 7-pin flat plug (standard Australian)
- [ ] Plug securely attached to trailer
- [ ] No corrosion on pins
- [ ] Gasket/seal in good condition
- [ ] Test plug continuity

---

## 3. Trailer Structure & Frame

### Frame Inspection
- [ ] No visible cracks in frame
- [ ] No rust or corrosion (structural)
- [ ] Welds intact and sound
- [ ] Cross members secure
- [ ] Paint/coating in good condition

### Axle & Suspension
- [ ] Axle straight (no bending)
- [ ] Springs in good condition
- [ ] Shackles and hangers secure
- [ ] Shock absorbers (if fitted) operational
- [ ] U-bolts tight and secure
- [ ] No play in suspension components

---

## 4. Wheels & Tyres

### Tyre Condition
- [ ] Tread depth minimum 1.5mm (legal minimum)
- [ ] No cuts, bulges, or damage
- [ ] Age of tyres acceptable (check date code)
- [ ] Spare tyre in good condition
- [ ] Tyres appropriate load rating

### Tyre Pressure
- [ ] Tyres inflated to correct pressure
- [ ] Check cold pressure before towing
- [ ] Valve caps fitted
- [ ] No slow leaks

### Wheel Bearings
- [ ] Bearings greased and serviced
- [ ] No play in wheel bearing
- [ ] Hub caps secure
- [ ] No overheating after use
- [ ] Bearing buddies installed (if applicable)
- [ ] Service annually or every 10,000km

### Wheels & Rims
- [ ] Rims not bent or damaged
- [ ] All wheel nuts present and tight
- [ ] Correct wheel nut torque
- [ ] Studs in good condition

---

## 5. Braking System

### Trailer Brakes (if required - ATM >750kg)
- [ ] Brakes operational on both wheels (if fitted)
- [ ] Brake shoes/pads have adequate life
- [ ] Brake cables not frayed
- [ ] Handbrake/override system functional
- [ ] Hydraulic brakes (if fitted) no leaks
- [ ] Test brake effectiveness

### Breakaway System (if ATM >2000kg)
- [ ] Breakaway brake system fitted
- [ ] Cable/chain in good condition
- [ ] Battery charged (electric brakes)
- [ ] System tested and functional

**Note:** Queensland law requires brakes on trailers with ATM over 750kg

---

## 6. Coupling & Hitch

### Coupling
- [ ] Coupling suitable for trailer weight
- [ ] Coupling ball size correct (usually 50mm)
- [ ] Locking mechanism works correctly
- [ ] Safety latch/pin engaged
- [ ] No wear or cracks
- [ ] Grease applied

### Safety Chains/Cables
- [ ] Two safety chains/cables fitted
- [ ] Attached to trailer frame (not axle)
- [ ] Chains cross under coupling
- [ ] Chain length appropriate (not dragging)
- [ ] Rated for trailer weight
- [ ] Shackles/links in good condition

### Jockey Wheel
- [ ] Jockey wheel clamp secure
- [ ] Wheel rotates freely
- [ ] Handle/winder operational
- [ ] Raises/lowers smoothly

---

## 7. Rollers & Supports

### Boat Support System
- [ ] All rollers rotate freely
- [ ] Roller bearings not seized
- [ ] Keel rollers aligned correctly
- [ ] Side/bow support rollers present
- [ ] No damaged or missing rollers
- [ ] Bow stop/winch post secure
- [ ] Rubber supports not deteriorated

---

## 8. Winch & Tie-Downs

### Winch
- [ ] Winch securely mounted
- [ ] Winch strap/cable in good condition
- [ ] No fraying or wear
- [ ] Winch operates smoothly
- [ ] Ratchet mechanism functional
- [ ] Hook/attachment point secure
- [ ] Safety catch on hook works

### Tie-Downs
- [ ] Transom tie-downs fitted
- [ ] Bow tie-down secure
- [ ] Straps in good condition
- [ ] Buckles/ratchets operational
- [ ] Boat secured at multiple points

---

## 9. Weight & Loading

### Capacity Ratings
- [ ] Know trailer ATM (Aggregate Trailer Mass)
- [ ] Know trailer GTM (Gross Trailer Mass)
- [ ] Boat + trailer within tow vehicle capacity
- [ ] Correct tow ball download (10-15% of trailer weight)
- [ ] Load distributed evenly

### Loading
- [ ] Boat centered on trailer
- [ ] Weight balanced side-to-side
- [ ] All gear secured
- [ ] No loose items
- [ ] Weight distribution correct (slight nose weight)

---

## 10. Number Plate

- [ ] Number plate securely attached
- [ ] Plate clean and legible
- [ ] Plate light illuminates plate
- [ ] Matches registration details

---

## 11. General Maintenance

### Regular Checks
- [ ] Grease all grease points
- [ ] Check all bolts and fasteners
- [ ] Inspect for rust/corrosion
- [ ] Clean and wash trailer after saltwater use
- [ ] Touch up paint on scratches
- [ ] Store on blocks (not tyres) if stored long-term

### Saltwater Use
- [ ] Rinse thoroughly after each saltwater use
- [ ] Flush wheel bearings
- [ ] Check for corrosion
- [ ] Inspect electrical connections
- [ ] Consider galvanizing or protective coating

---

## 12. Towing Vehicle Requirements

### Vehicle Capacity
- [ ] Check vehicle towing capacity
- [ ] Ensure within GVM and GCM limits
- [ ] Correct tow ball fitted (rated)
- [ ] Tow bar professionally installed
- [ ] Trailer plug socket fitted and working

### Towing Mirrors
- [ ] Extended mirrors fitted (if required)
- [ ] Can see along both sides of trailer
- [ ] Mirrors secure and adjusted

---

## 13. Pre-Travel Checklist

**Before Every Trip:**
- [ ] All lights working
- [ ] Tyres inflated correctly
- [ ] Wheel nuts tight
- [ ] Coupling locked and secure
- [ ] Safety chains attached and crossed
- [ ] Boat secured with straps
- [ ] Winch strap tensioned
- [ ] Drain plug removed from boat
- [ ] Jockey wheel raised and secured
- [ ] Load balanced and secure
- [ ] Check nothing dragging

---

## 14. Documentation Required

### On-Road Requirements
- [ ] Trailer registration papers
- [ ] Tow vehicle registration
- [ ] Driver's license
- [ ] Insurance documents (recommended)
- [ ] Roadworthy certificate (if required)

---

## Maintenance Schedule

### Every Trip
- Check lights, tyres, coupling, straps

### Every 3 Months or 5,000km
- Check/tighten wheel nuts
- Inspect brakes
- Grease coupling

### Every 6 Months or 10,000km
- Service wheel bearings
- Full inspection of frame and structure
- Check all fasteners

### Annually
- Professional inspection (recommended)
- Replace old tyres (5+ years)
- Full bearing repack
- Brake service

---

## Penalties & Enforcement

**Defective Trailer Penalty:** Up to $4,135 fine (QLD)
**Unregistered Trailer:** Fines and potential impound
**Non-compliant equipment:** On-the-spot fines
