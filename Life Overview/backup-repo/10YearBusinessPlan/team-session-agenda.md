## SESSION GROUND RULES

### Effective Collaboration Principles

1. **No rank in the room**: All voices are equal during this session. The best idea wins, regardless of who suggests it.
2. **Start on time, end on time**: Respect everyone's time by maintaining the schedule.
3. **One conversation at a time**: No side discussions or devices (laptops closed except for note-taking).
4. **Disagree and commit**: Healthy debate is encouraged, but once a decision is made, everyone commits fully.
5. **Focus on the business**: Leave personal frustrations at the door; focus on what's best for Venturer Technology.
6. **Action-oriented mindset**: We're here to make decisions and create accountability, not just discuss.

### Decision-Making Protocol

**When the team disagrees:**
1. **First**: Discuss pros/cons openly (5 minutes max per issue)
2. **Second**: If no consensus, Rick makes the call (Visionary has final say on strategic priorities)
3. **Third**: Everyone commits to the decision, even if it wasn't their preference
4. **Fourth**: Track the decision as an experiment; revisit in 30-60 days if needed

**Parking Lot for Off-Topic Items:**
- Kristyn maintains a "Parking Lot" list for important topics that arise but are outside this session's scope
- These items will be added to the Issues List or scheduled for future discussion

### PART 2: SET Q1 2026 ROCKS (60 minutes)

#### What Are Rocks? (10 minutes)
**Facilitator**: Rick

**Explanation**:
> "Rocks are the 3-7 MOST IMPORTANT priorities for the next 90 days. They're called 'Rocks' because of Stephen Covey's analogy: if you fill a jar with sand first (small daily tasks), you can't fit the big rocks (important priorities). But if you put the big rocks in first, the sand fills in around them.
>
> In EOS, Rocks are our forcing function for prioritization. We can't do everything at once, but we CAN do 3-7 critical things every 90 days."

**Rock Criteria (MUST meet ALL of these):**
1. **90-Day Timeframe**: Completable within Q1 2026 (January 1 - March 31)
2. **Specific & Measurable**: Crystal clear when it's "done" (binary yes/no completion)
3. **Assigned Owner**: ONE person responsible (not shared ownership)
4. **Company or Individual**: Either supports company priorities OR supports role responsibilities
5. **High Impact**: Moves us meaningfully toward 1-Year Plan goals

**Examples of GOOD Rocks:**
- ‚úÖ "Profit First Implementation Complete" (Owner: Kristyn, Done by: March 31)
- ‚úÖ "First Hire Recruited and Offer Extended" (Owner: Rick, Done by: February 28)
- ‚úÖ "5 Core Processes Documented Using Template" (Owner: Kristyn, Done by: March 31)
- ‚úÖ "10 Consecutive Weekly L10 Meetings Completed" (Owner: Rick, Done by: March 31)

**Examples of BAD Rocks:**
- ‚ùå "Improve customer service" (Not measurable, no completion criteria)
- ‚ùå "Grow revenue" (Ongoing activity, not specific 90-day outcome)
- ‚ùå "Work on marketing" (Vague, no clear definition of "done")

### PART 5: ESTABLISH MEETING PULSE (20 minutes)
**Timing**: 11:45am - 12:05pm

#### What Is the Meeting Pulse? (3 minutes)
**Facilitator**: Rick

**Explanation**:
> "The Meeting Pulse is our commitment to WEEKLY accountability. In EOS, the Level 10 Meeting is the heartbeat that keeps everything else alive. It's 90 minutes, same day/time every week, with a strict agenda that ensures we review our numbers, track our Rocks, and solve our most important Issues.
>
> The name 'Level 10' comes from the goal: every participant should rate the meeting a 10 out of 10 for productivity and effectiveness. If meetings consistently score below 8, something needs to change about the agenda, attendance, or facilitation."

**Why Weekly Matters:**
- Monthly is too slow - problems compound for 4 weeks before discussion
- Daily is too frequent - no time for action between meetings
- Weekly creates rhythm: commit on Monday, execute all week, report next Monday

#### Define L10 Meeting Structure (7 minutes)
**Facilitator**: Rick
**Activity**: Review standard EOS L10 agenda

**Standard Level 10 Meeting Agenda** (90 minutes total):

**1. SEGUE (5 minutes)**
- Personal and professional good news
- Purpose: Start meeting on positive note, build team connection
- Each person shares one good thing from past week (personal or business)

**2. SCORECARD REVIEW (5 minutes)**
- Review each metric: on track (green), slight concern (yellow), or problem (red)
- Purpose: Spot trends and early warnings
- NO discussion or problem-solving here - just identify red/yellow items
- Red/yellow items get added to Issues List for IDS later

**3. ROCK REVIEW (5 minutes)**
- Each Rock owner reports: on track (green), at risk (yellow), or off track (red)
- Purpose: Ensure 90-day priorities stay visible and accountable
- NO discussion here - just status update
- Off-track Rocks get added to Issues List for IDS

**4. CUSTOMER/EMPLOYEE HEADLINES (5 minutes)**
- Share important updates: customer wins/losses, employee changes, key developments
- Purpose: Keep everyone informed on critical people issues
- Brief headlines only - detailed discussion goes to Issues List

**5. TO-DO LIST REVIEW (5 minutes)**
- Review last week's commitments: done or not done (binary)
- Purpose: Create accountability for commitments made in previous L10
- NOT the place for excuses - just report status
- Incomplete to-dos get added to Issues List if still relevant

**6. IDS - IDENTIFY, DISCUSS, SOLVE (60 minutes)**
- Work through Issues List using IDS framework
- Purpose: Solve the most important problems/opportunities facing the business
- **Identify**: Prioritize top 3-5 Issues for this week's meeting
- **Discuss**: Get to root cause (not just symptoms) - "What's the REAL issue here?"
- **Solve**: Create specific action items with ownership and deadlines ‚Üí Next week's To-Do List

**7. CONCLUDE (5 minutes)**
- Recap To-Dos for next week (who owns what by when)
- Cascade messages: what needs to be communicated to broader team?
- Rate the meeting 1-10: "How effective was this meeting?"
- Purpose: Ensure clarity and commitment before leaving

**Total**: 90 minutes (use timer to stay on track!)

## FACILITATION TIPS FOR RICK

### Managing Group Dynamics

**If discussion gets off-track:**
- "That's a great topic for the Parking Lot / Issues List. Let's capture it and keep moving."
- "We're spending a lot of time on this. Is it worth making this a Rock, or can we solve it quickly in IDS during an L10?"

**If someone dominates conversation:**
- "Thanks for that perspective. Let's hear from Kristyn/Dean on this."
- Use round-robin format: "Let's go around the table - Rick, then Kristyn, then Dean."

**If team is stuck deciding between options:**
- "Let's each vote on our top choice. If we're split, I'll make the call and we'll commit to trying it for one quarter."
- "What would need to be true for Option A to be the right choice? What about Option B?"

**If energy drops mid-session:**
- Take an unscheduled 5-minute break
- Do a quick physical activity (stand and stretch, walk outside for 2 minutes)
- Shift from analytical discussion to creative brainstorming

### Keeping Session on Schedule

**Use these time reminders:**
- "We have 10 minutes left for this activity. Let's focus on decisions."
- "We're running 5 minutes behind. We can either extend the session or move faster through the next section. Preferences?"
- "This is great discussion, but we need to move to the next topic to finish on time."

**If you're running significantly behind (>15 minutes):**
- Pause and ask team: "Do we extend by 30 minutes, or do we prioritize and cut something?"
- Option 1: Extend session if energy is high and we're making great progress
- Option 2: Cut Issues List to 15 minutes (we can add Issues during L10 meetings)
- Option 3: Simplify Scorecard to 5-7 metrics instead of 10-15 (can expand later)

### Encouraging Full Participation

**To draw out quieter participants:**
- "Kristyn, you've been quiet on this topic. What's your take?"
- "Dean, from your operational perspective, what would make the biggest difference?"
- Direct questions: "Kristyn, which of these financial metrics would give you the most peace of mind?"

**To ensure psychological safety:**
- "There are no bad ideas in brainstorming. Let's get everything on the table."
- "We're not committing to anything yet - just exploring options."
- When someone disagrees: "That's valuable dissent. Tell us more about your concerns."

### Handling Disagreement

**When Rick and Kristyn disagree (Visionary vs. Integrator tension):**
- Acknowledge both perspectives: "Rick's growth focus and Kristyn's profit discipline are BOTH right. How do we honor both?"
- Look for synthesis: "Is there a third option that achieves growth AND maintains margin?"
- Make it explicit: "This is healthy Visionary/Integrator tension. We need both viewpoints."

**When consensus isn't emerging:**
- "We've discussed this for 10 minutes. Let's use our decision protocol: I'll make the call, and we all commit to trying it for Q1. We can revisit at the quarterly review."
