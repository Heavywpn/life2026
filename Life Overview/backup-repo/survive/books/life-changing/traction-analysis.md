## The Transformation

### What Changed Specifically?
Traction introduced the **Entrepreneurial Operating System (EOS)**, a complete framework for running a business (or managing personal projects) with clarity, discipline, and accountability. The system transformed chaotic, reactive operations into structured, proactive systems through **six key components** and practical tools like Rocks (90-day goals), Scorecards (weekly metrics), and L10 Meetings (structured problem-solving).

**Behavioral Changes**:
- Implemented **Rocks** (quarterly 90-day priorities) for focused execution
- Created **Scorecard** (5-15 weekly metrics) for objective business pulse
- Ran **L10 Meetings** (90-minute structured weekly meetings) for issue resolution
- Applied **IDS** (Identify, Discuss, Solve) to problem-solving systematically
- Used **Vision/Traction Organizer (V/TO)** for strategic planning
- Shifted from reactive firefighting to proactive systems thinking
- **Personal application**: Applied EOS principles to survival project goal-setting

### Before vs. After Comparison

**Before:**
- Chaotic, reactive operations (firefighting mode)
- Vague goals ("grow the business," "get better at X")

## Core Insights

### 1. The Six Key Components (EOS Model)

**The Framework**: Every business has six key components. When they're all strong and aligned, you have a great business. When weak or misaligned, you have frustration and stagnation.

**The Six Components**:

**1. Vision** - Everyone 100% on the same page with where you're going and how to get there
- Tool: Vision/Traction Organizer (V/TO) - 8 questions answered on 2 pages
- Core values, core focus, 10-year target, marketing strategy, 3-year picture, 1-year plan, quarterly Rocks, issues list

**2. People** - Right people in right seats
- Tool: Accountability Chart (not org chart) - who's accountable for what
- GWC: Gets it, Wants it, has Capacity to do it
- People Analyzer: Score people against core values + GWC

**3. Data** - Objective numbers-based pulse on the business
- Tool: Scorecard - 5-15 weekly metrics (leading indicators)
- Weekly review prevents surprises, spots trends early
- Not lagging indicators (financials) - leading indicators (activity metrics)

**4. Issues** - Identify, prioritize, and systematically solve problems
- Tool: Issues List + IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve)
- All issues captured in one place, prioritized, solved systematically
- Root cause resolution, not Band-Aid fixes

**5. Process** - Core processes documented and followed by all (FBA)
- Tool: Core process documentation
- Identify 3-7 core processes, document each, ensure everyone follows
- Consistency, scalability, delegation

**6. Traction** - Discipline and accountability to execute and achieve goals
- Tools: Rocks (90-day priorities), Meeting Pulse (L10 weekly meetings), To-Dos
- Execution discipline, measurable results, accountability

**Why This Matters**:
- **Holistic system**: Not just strategy OR execution - both, integrated
- **Simplicity**: Six components, simple tools, easy to implement
- **Proven**: Used by 250,000+ businesses worldwide
- **Transferable**: Principles apply to business, personal projects, family goals

**Application to Survival Project**:
- **Vision**: Survival project goals (TELOS-like clarity)
- **People**: Accountability to self, family involvement
- **Data**: Track field test results, skill progression, gear inventory
- **Issues**: Systematic problem-solving (why did fire fail? IDS it)
- **Process**: Document core survival skills step-by-step
- **Traction**: Quarterly Rocks for survival goals, weekly review

### 2. Rocks: The Power of 90-Day Priorities

**The Concept**: Set 3-7 most important priorities for the next 90 days. Focus only on these. At end of 90 days, 80%+ should be complete.

**Why 90 Days?**:
- Long enough to accomplish meaningful work
- Short enough to maintain focus and urgency
- Matches seasonal rhythms (quarterly planning)
- Prevents "lost year" syndrome (annual goals abandoned by February)

**Rocks vs. Goals**:
- **Rocks** = specific, measurable, 90-day focused
- **Goals** = vague, unmeasurable, annual wish list
- Example Rock: "Complete DiSSS fire-making progression (ferro rod to bow drill) by Dec 31"
- Example Goal: "Get better at fire-making" (vague, no deadline, unmeasurable)

**The Process**:
1. Quarterly planning: Review last quarter, set new Rocks for next 90 days
2. Weekly review: Track Rock progress in L10 meetings
3. End of quarter: Rate completion (80%+ = on track)
4. Adjust and repeat

**Why This Matters**:
- **Focus**: 3-7 priorities vs. 47 "important" things
- **Measurable**: Clear completion criteria (done or not done)
- **Accountability**: Quarterly reviews create natural checkpoints
- **Momentum**: 90-day wins compound over time

**Application to Survival Project**:
- **Q4 2025 Rocks**:
  1. Complete fire-making mastery (all 6 methods)
  2. Build 30-day food storage (Slow-Carb compliant)
  3. Finish shelter-building field guide (one-page compressed reference)
  4. Hunt and field-dress first small game (rabbit or squirrel)
  5. Create 10 one-page skill references (DiSSS compressed)
- **Weekly review**: Track Rock progress during weekly planning
- **Quarterly cadence**: Plan (week 1), execute (weeks 2-12), review (week 13), repeat

### 3. L10 Meetings: The 90-Minute Discipline

**The Concept**: Same day, same time, 90 minutes, every week. Structured agenda. Solve 2-5 issues per meeting. Rate meeting effectiveness 1-10 (goal: Level 10).

**The L10 Agenda** (90 minutes total):
1. **Segue** (5 min): Good news, personal/professional
2. **Scorecard** (5 min): Review 5-15 weekly metrics, identify off-track items
3. **Rock Review** (5 min): Update progress on quarterly Rocks (on/off track)
4. **Customer/Employee Headlines** (5 min): Quick updates (no discussion, just FYI)
5. **To-Do List** (5 min): Review last week's to-dos (done or not done)
6. **IDS** (60 min): Identify, Discuss, Solve - tackle 2-5 issues from Issues List
7. **Conclude** (5 min): Recap to-dos, cascading messages, rate meeting 1-10

**Why This Matters**:
- **Structure prevents rambling**: No more 2-hour meetings that solve nothing
- **60 minutes on IDS**: Majority of time solving actual problems (not reporting)
- **Same agenda every week**: Eliminates "what should we talk about?" paralysis
- **Rating creates accountability**: Goal is Level 10 (forces improvement)

**Key Rules**:
- **Start on time, end on time** (90 minutes, no exceptions)
- **No tech distractions** (phones/laptops down during IDS)
- **Issues List is king** (all problems go here, prioritized, tackled systematically)
- **To-dos get done** (or dropped - no eternal to-do list)

**Application to Survival Project**:
- **Solo L10** (personal weekly review):
  - Segue: Wins from past week
  - Scorecard: Review metrics (training sessions completed, skills practiced, gear acquired)
  - Rock Review: Progress on quarterly Rocks
  - Headlines: Key updates (weather forecast for field test, new gear available)
  - To-Dos: Last week's commitments (completed or dropped)
  - IDS: Solve 1-3 issues (why did shelter fail? what's blocking fire mastery?)
  - Conclude: Next week's to-dos, rate effectiveness
- **Family L10** (if involving family in preparedness):
  - Same structure, 60-90 minutes weekly
  - Everyone reports Rock progress
  - Solve family preparedness issues together

### 4. IDS: Identify, Discuss, Solve (Systematic Problem-Solving)

**The Three-Step Process**:

**I - Identify**: What's the REAL issue? (Not symptoms, root cause)
- Example: "We keep running out of firewood" â†’ Real issue: "No system for estimating wood needed per night"
- Get below surface level ("sales are down" â†’ why? â†’ lead gen broken? closing rate? pricing?)

**D - Discuss**: Talk it through until root cause is crystal clear
- All perspectives heard
- Data reviewed (if available)
- Root cause identified (not just symptoms)
- Example: "We don't have a wood estimation system because we've never tracked burn rates by temperature"

**S - Solve**: Permanent solution with clear owner and deadline
- Not Band-Aid ("just gather more wood")
- Permanent fix ("Create wood burn rate chart by temp, use to calculate nightly needs")
- Clear owner ("Rick owns this")
- Clear deadline ("Complete by next weekend field test")
- To-do created, tracked in next L10

**Why This Matters**:
- **Prevents recurring issues**: Solve root cause, not symptoms
- **Creates ownership**: Someone owns the solution (not "we should...")
- **Measurable**: To-do has deadline, gets done or doesn't
- **Systematic**: Same process every time (no reinventing problem-solving)

**Common Mistakes**:
- âŒ **Jumping to solutions** before identifying root cause
- âŒ **Talking too long** without solving (analysis paralysis)
- âŒ **No owner assigned** ("we should..." = nobody does it)
- âŒ **Band-Aid fixes** that fail within weeks

**Application to Survival Project**:
- **Field test failures**: IDS every failure (fire didn't start â†’ why? â†’ tinder too wet? technique wrong? â†’ solve permanently)
- **Skill plateaus**: IDS why progress stalled (bow drill not working â†’ root cause? â†’ spindle pressure? wood selection? â†’ permanent fix)
- **Gear failures**: IDS equipment issues (water filter clogged â†’ why? â†’ pre-filtering skipped? â†’ create pre-filter SOP)
- **Goal misses**: IDS why Rock not completed (shelter guide incomplete â†’ why? â†’ scope too large? â†’ break into smaller Rocks next quarter)

### Framework 3: IDS Applied to Field Test Failures

**Scenario**: Fire-making field test failed (couldn't start fire with bow drill)

**I - Identify**:
- **Surface issue**: "Bow drill didn't work"
- **Dig deeper**: What specifically failed? (no smoke? smoke but no coal? coal but didn't catch?)
- **Root cause**: "Spindle pressure inconsistent - hand slipped on bearing block, lost downward force"

**D - Discuss**:
- Review technique (video yourself, compare to expert demonstration)
- Check equipment (wood selection, spindle diameter, fireboard notch depth)
- Environmental factors (humidity, wood moisture, temperature)
- **Conclusion**: Hand bearing block technique insufficient - need better grip or switch to socket bearing

**S - Solve**:
- **Permanent solution**: "Create socket bearing from stone with natural depression OR master hand position with cloth pad for grip"
- **Owner**: Rick
- **Deadline**: Test both solutions by next weekend field test
- **To-Do**: "Practice bow drill with socket bearing + hand position with pad, 30 min daily Mon-Fri, field test Sat"
- **Success criteria**: Achieve coal in <5 min, 80% success rate over 5 attempts

**Next L10**:
- Review to-do (done or not done)
- If done, document success rate, update one-page reference
- If not done, IDS why (too ambitious? wrong solution? not prioritized? â†’ solve)

**Apply to Any Failure**:
- Shelter collapsed â†’ IDS (root cause: structure design? material selection? weather? â†’ permanent fix)
- Water purification failed â†’ IDS (filter clogged? technique wrong? water too turbid? â†’ permanent fix)
- Navigation error â†’ IDS (map reading? compass declination? pacing count? â†’ permanent fix)
- Hunt unsuccessful â†’ IDS (scent control? noise discipline? timing? location? â†’ permanent fix)
