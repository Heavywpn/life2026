## The Gary Brecka Protocol: Test, Identify, Optimize

### Step 1: Testing (Know Your Baseline)

**Core Philosophy:** Guessing at supplementation is wasteful and potentially harmful. Test to know exactly what YOUR body needs.

**Essential Tests:**

**1. Comprehensive Blood Panel**
- Complete Metabolic Panel (CMP)
- Complete Blood Count (CBC)
- Lipid Panel (cholesterol, triglycerides)
- Thyroid (TSH, Free T3, Free T4, Reverse T3)
- Vitamin D (25-hydroxyvitamin D)
- Vitamin B12 (serum B12)
- Folate (serum folate)
- Magnesium (RBC magnesium, NOT serum)
- **Homocysteine** (KEY marker for methylation)
- hs-CRP (high-sensitivity C-reactive protein for inflammation)
- HbA1c (blood sugar regulation)
- Iron panel (ferritin, iron, TIBC)

**Cost:** $100-300 depending on provider
**Frequency:** Every 6-12 months

**2. Genetic Testing (MTHFR Focus)**
- MTHFR gene (C677T variant, A1298C variant)
- Other methylation genes (COMT, MTR, MTRR)
- 10X Health System offers this (Gary's company)
- 23andMe raw data can be analyzed

**Cost:** $300-500 for comprehensive panel
**Frequency:** Once (genetics don't change)

**3. Advanced Testing (Optional)**
- Hormone panel (testosterone, estrogen, cortisol, DHEA)
- Heavy metals (hair or urine test)
- Gut microbiome (stool test)
- Food sensitivities (IgG testing - controversial)
- Organic acids test
- DUTCH test (comprehensive hormones)

**Cost:** $200-600 per test
**Frequency:** As needed based on symptoms

### Step 2: Identify Deficiencies & Issues

**Common Findings (Per Gary Brecka):**

**1. Methylation Issues (50-60% of population)**
- MTHFR gene variants
- Elevated homocysteine (>7 μmol/L)
- Can't convert folic acid → active folate
- B12 absorption issues
- Symptoms: Fatigue, brain fog, depression, anxiety

**2. Vitamin D Deficiency (80% of population)**
- Optimal: 50-80 ng/mL (not just "normal")
- Most people: 20-30 ng/mL (deficient)
- Symptoms: Fatigue, poor immunity, mood issues

**3. Magnesium Deficiency (75% of population)**
- Need RBC magnesium test (not serum)
- Critical for 300+ enzymatic reactions
- Symptoms: Sleep issues, muscle cramps, anxiety, fatigue

**4. B12 Deficiency (Common)**
- Especially with MTHFR variants
- Even "normal" B12 may be insufficient
- Symptoms: Fatigue, neuropathy, cognitive decline

**5. Inflammation (Chronic)**
- hs-CRP >1.0 mg/L indicates inflammation
- Root causes: Diet, stress, toxins, infections
- Silent killer for long-term health

### Step 3: Supplement Strategically

**Gary Brecka's Key Principle:** Only supplement what you're actually deficient in based on testing.

**Common Deficiency Protocols:**

**Methylation Support (if MTHFR positive + high homocysteine):**
- **Methylated B12** (methylcobalamin) - 1000-5000 mcg
  - NOT cyanocobalamin (synthetic, requires methylation)
- **Methylated Folate** (5-MTHF / L-methylfolate) - 400-1000 mcg
  - NOT folic acid (synthetic, blocks folate receptors)
- **B6** (P5P form) - 25-50 mg
- **Riboflavin** (B2) - 100-400 mg
- **Trimethylglycine** (TMG / Betaine) - 500-2000 mg

**Goal:** Lower homocysteine below 7 μmol/L

**Vitamin D Protocol (if deficient):**
- **Vitamin D3** - 5,000-10,000 IU daily (based on blood levels)
- **Vitamin K2** (MK-7) - 100-200 mcg (directs calcium properly)
- **Magnesium** - Required for D3 activation

**Goal:** Maintain 50-80 ng/mL year-round

**Magnesium Protocol (if deficient):**
- **Magnesium Glycinate** - 400-600 mg before bed (sleep, relaxation)
- **Magnesium Threonate** - 2000 mg (brain health, cognitive)
- **Magnesium Malate** - 1200 mg (energy, daytime)

**Goal:** Improve sleep, reduce cramps, enhance recovery

**Foundational Stack (After Testing):**
- Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) - 2-4g daily
- Creatine Monohydrate - 5g daily (cognitive + physical performance)
- Electrolytes - Daily (sodium, potassium, magnesium)
- Vitamin D3 + K2 - Based on blood levels
- Methylated B-Complex - If MTHFR positive

## Methylation Deep Dive

*Gary Brecka's Core Focus Area*

### What is Methylation?

**Definition:** Methylation is the process of adding a methyl group (CH3) to molecules throughout the body. It's involved in:
- DNA repair
- Detoxification
- Neurotransmitter production (dopamine, serotonin, norepinephrine)
- Hormone metabolism
- Immune function
- Energy production

**Simple Explanation:** Think of methylation as an on/off switch for genes and cellular processes. Proper methylation = everything works. Poor methylation = cascading problems.

### The MTHFR Gene

**What It Does:** MTHFR gene produces enzyme that converts folate into active form (5-MTHF) needed for methylation.

**Common Variants:**
- **C677T** - Most common, reduces enzyme function 30-70%
- **A1298C** - Less severe, reduces function 20-30%
- **Compound Heterozygous** - One of each variant, moderate impact
- **Homozygous** - Two copies of same variant, severe impact

**Prevalence:** 50-60% of population has at least one variant

**Impact:**
- Can't efficiently convert folic acid → active folate
- Elevated homocysteine (cardiovascular risk)
- Neurotransmitter imbalances
- Detoxification issues
- Energy production problems

### Testing for MTHFR

**Option 1: Direct Genetic Test**
- 10X Health System (Gary's company)
- Other genetic testing services
- Cost: $300-500

**Option 2: 23andMe + Raw Data Analysis**
- Get 23andMe ancestry test
- Download raw data
- Upload to sites like Promethease or Genetic Genie
- Search for MTHFR variants

**Option 3: Functional Test (Homocysteine)**
- Elevated homocysteine (>7 μmol/L) suggests methylation issues
- Blood test, widely available
- Cost: $50-100
- Not definitive but good screening

### Treating MTHFR Issues

**Protocol:**
1. **Stop Synthetic Folic Acid** - Read labels, avoid fortified foods
2. **Supplement Methylated Folate** - 400-1000 mcg 5-MTHF
3. **Supplement Methylated B12** - 1000-5000 mcg methylcobalamin
4. **Add B6 (P5P)** - 25-50 mg
5. **Add B2 (Riboflavin)** - 100-400 mg (supports MTHFR enzyme)
6. **Monitor Homocysteine** - Retest in 3-6 months

**Expected Results:**
- Energy improvement in 2-4 weeks
- Brain fog clears
- Mood stabilizes
- Homocysteine drops below 7

**Gary's Experience:** Many clients with "treatment-resistant depression" or chronic fatigue resolve with methylation support.

## Common Health Issues & Biohacking Solutions

### Issue: Chronic Fatigue

**Test:**
- Thyroid panel (TSH, Free T3, Free T4, Reverse T3)
- B12, folate, ferritin
- Homocysteine
- Cortisol (morning and evening)
- Blood sugar (HbA1c, fasting glucose)

**Likely Causes:**
- Hypothyroidism or subclinical hypothyroidism
- B12/folate deficiency
- Iron deficiency (especially women)
- Chronic stress (HPA axis dysfunction)
- Poor sleep quality
- Methylation issues

**Protocol:**
- Address deficiencies (methylated Bs, iron if needed)
- Thyroid support if needed (work with doctor)
- Sleep optimization (magnesium, cool room, consistent schedule)
- Stress management (cold therapy, meditation, adaptogenic herbs)
- Eliminate inflammatory foods

### Issue: Brain Fog / Cognitive Decline

**Test:**
- Homocysteine (methylation)
- Thyroid panel
- Vitamin D
- B12
- Fasting insulin (brain insulin resistance)

**Likely Causes:**
- Methylation issues (can't make neurotransmitters)
- Hypothyroidism
- Insulin resistance
- Chronic inflammation
- Poor sleep

**Protocol:**
- Methylation support (5-MTHF, methylated B12)
- Magnesium threonate (crosses blood-brain barrier)
- Creatine (5g daily - cognitive benefits)
- Omega-3 (2-4g EPA/DHA)
- Eliminate sugar/processed carbs
- Cold plunging (dopamine, mental clarity)

### Issue: Poor Sleep

**Test:**
- Magnesium (RBC)
- Cortisol (evening - should be low)
- Sleep study if snoring/apnea suspected

**Likely Causes:**
- Magnesium deficiency (most common)
- High evening cortisol (stress)
- Mouth breathing (sleep apnea)
- Blue light exposure
- Room too warm
- Inconsistent schedule

**Protocol:**
- **Magnesium glycinate** - 400-600mg before bed
- **Mouth tape** - Forces nasal breathing
- **Cool room** - 60-67°F
- **Blue light blockers** - 2 hours before bed
- **Consistent schedule** - Same time every night
- **Morning sunlight** - Regulates circadian rhythm
- **No caffeine after 2pm**

### Issue: Anxiety / Depression

**Test:**
- Homocysteine (methylation → neurotransmitters)
- Thyroid panel (thyroid affects mood dramatically)
- Vitamin D
- Omega-3 levels
- Cortisol pattern

**Likely Causes:**
- Methylation issues (can't make serotonin/dopamine)
- Chronic inflammation
- Vitamin D deficiency
- Omega-3 deficiency
- HPA axis dysfunction (chronic stress)
- Gut issues (gut-brain axis)

**Protocol:**
- **Methylation support** - 5-MTHF, methylated B12, P5P
- **Omega-3** - 2-4g EPA/DHA (EPA for depression)
- **Vitamin D** - Optimize to 50-80 ng/mL
- **Magnesium** - Calming effect
- **Cold plunging** - Dopamine/norepinephrine boost
- **Exercise** - Resistance + cardio
- **Sunlight exposure** - Daily
- **Eliminate inflammatory foods**

### Issue: Low Energy / Motivation

**Test:**
- Testosterone (men) / Estrogen & progesterone (women)
- Thyroid panel
- Cortisol pattern
- B vitamins
- Iron/ferritin

**Likely Causes:**
- Hormone imbalances
- Thyroid issues
- Adrenal fatigue (HPA axis dysfunction)
- Methylation issues
- Chronic stress

**Protocol:**
- **Cold plunging** - Dopamine boost (2-3 hours)
- **Creatine** - 5g daily (energy + motivation)
- **Methylated B-complex** - If MTHFR positive
- **Sleep optimization** - Energy foundation
