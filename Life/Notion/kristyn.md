## Introduction (1 minute)

Before we dive into the main topics, let's address three of the most common password mistakes made by businesses using online applications:

- **Using weak or default passwords:** Many businesses fail to change default passwords or use simple, easily guessable passwords for their accounts. This makes it trivially easy for hackers to gain unauthorized access.
- **Password reuse across multiple accounts:** Employees often use the same password for various business applications. If one account is compromised, all others with the same password become vulnerable.
- **Lack of regular password updates:** Businesses frequently neglect to implement and enforce policies for regular password changes. This increases the risk of long-term unauthorized access if a password is compromised.

Understanding these common mistakes highlights the importance of robust password management and security practices, which we'll explore in detail throughout this presentation.

## Password Managers (3 minutes)

### What are Password Managers?

Password managers are tools that securely store and manage your passwords for various online accounts.

Picture this: You're a secret agent with a mission to protect valuable information. But instead of a high-tech gadget, your most powerful tool is... a password manager. These digital vaults are like having a personal bodyguard for your online credentials. They're not just convenient; they're the unsung heroes of our digital lives.

Think of a password manager as your own Fort Knox for the internet age. It's a secure digital safe where you can store all your passwords, each one as unique and complex as a snowflake. No more sticky notes with passwords plastered around your monitor or trying to remember if it was "Fluffy123!" or "Fluffy321!" for your bank account.

But password managers aren't just about remembering passwords. They're about revolutionizing your online security. Imagine having a different, virtually unbreakable password for every single account you own, without the headache of trying to recall them all. That's the power of a password manager.

### Why are they important?

- They allow you to use strong, unique passwords for each account
- Reduce the risk of password reuse
- Protect against credential stuffing attacks
- Enhance security by automatically generating and filling in complex passwords

A credential stuffing attack is a type of cyberattack where attackers use stolen username and password pairs from one service to attempt to log in to other services. This attack exploits the common practice of password reuse across multiple accounts. Password managers help prevent this by encouraging the use of unique passwords for each service.

- Simplify the process of managing multiple accounts

  

### Revoking Passkey Access for Terminated Staff

Revoking passkey access for terminated staff members is a crucial aspect of maintaining security in a business environment. Here's how it typically works:

- **Centralized Management:** Businesses usually manage passkeys through a centralized identity provider or authentication server.
- **Account Deactivation:** Upon staff termination, the IT department deactivates the employee's account in the central system.
- **Revocation of Trust:** The authentication server revokes trust for that user's passkeys, rendering them invalid across all services.
- **Real-time Updates:** This change is propagated in real-time to all connected services and applications.
- **Device Management:** If passkeys are stored on company-owned devices, these devices should be wiped or reclaimed.

It's important to note that passkey revocation is generally more straightforward and immediate compared to managing multiple passwords across various systems. This is because passkeys are centrally managed and can be revoked instantly across all services.

Businesses should include passkey revocation in their offboarding processes to ensure consistent security practices. Regular audits of active passkeys and associated accounts can also help maintain system integrity.

### Popular Password Managers

- LastPass: Cloud-based, user-friendly interface, free and premium options
- 1Password: Strong security features, family and team plans available
- Dashlane: Includes VPN service, automatic password changer
- Bitwarden: Open-source, self-hosting option, affordable pricing

### Recommended Password Lengths

Current best practices recommend:

- Minimum of 12 characters for standard accounts
- 16 characters or more for high-security accounts
- Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters

Remember, longer passwords are generally more secure. With a password manager, you can use unique, complex passwords for each account without having to memorize them.

### Benefits for businesses

- Improved overall security posture
- Reduced risk of data breaches
- Increased productivity by eliminating password reset requests

  

### Onboarding and Offboarding Staff with Password Managers

Implementing password managers in your organization streamlines the onboarding and offboarding processes for employees. During onboarding, IT teams can quickly set up secure access to necessary accounts without sharing passwords directly. When offboarding, revoking access becomes a simple task, ensuring that departing employees no longer have access to sensitive information. This not only enhances security but also saves time and reduces the risk of overlooked access points.

## Multi-Factor Authentication (3 minutes)

### What is Multi-Factor Authentication?

MFA is a security process that requires users to provide two or more verification factors to gain access to an account or system.

### Types of MFA

- Something you know (password, PIN)
- Something you have (smartphone, security token)
- Something you are (biometrics like fingerprints or facial recognition)

### Why is MFA important?

- Adds an extra layer of security beyond passwords
- Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access
- Protects against phishing and social engineering attacks

### Benefits for businesses

- Enhanced protection of sensitive data
- Compliance with industry regulations
- Increased customer trust and confidence

## Cybersecurity Awareness (2 minutes)

### What is Cybersecurity Awareness?

Cybersecurity awareness involves educating employees and stakeholders about potential security threats and best practices to mitigate risks.

### Why is it important?

- Human error is a leading cause of security breaches
- Empowers employees to recognize and respond to threats
- Creates a culture of security within the organization

### Key topics for awareness training

- Phishing and social engineering
- Safe browsing habits
- Data protection and privacy
- Mobile device security
