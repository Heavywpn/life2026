## 1. INTRODUCTION AND INTERPRETATION

### 1.1 Purpose

This Master Service Agreement ("MSA" or "Agreement") establishes the legal framework governing the provision of managed IT services by the Provider to the Client.

### 1.2 Structure

The specific services, pricing, and service levels applicable to the Client are detailed in one or more Service Schedules attached to and forming part of this Agreement. In the event of any inconsistency between this MSA and a Service Schedule, the Service Schedule prevails to the extent of the inconsistency.

### 1.3 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with any Service Schedules and any documents expressly incorporated by reference, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements relating to its subject matter.

### 1.4 Definitions

In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires:

| Term | Definition |

| **Agreement** | This Master Service Agreement and all attached Service Schedules |
| **Business Day** | A day that is not a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in Brisbane, Queensland |

## 2. TERM AND RENEWAL

### 2.1 Initial Term

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for the Initial Term specified in the Service Schedule, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Agreement.

### 2.2 Renewal

After the Initial Term, this Agreement will automatically renew for successive periods equal to the Initial Term ("Renewal Terms") unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days before the end of the then-current term.

### 2.3 Effect of Expiry or Termination

Upon expiry or termination of this Agreement:
- (a) The Client must pay all outstanding Fees and charges;
- (b) Each party must return or destroy the other party's Confidential Information;
- (c) The Provider will provide reasonable transition assistance as described in Section 11.

---

## 3. SERVICES

### 3.1 Provision of Services

The Provider agrees to provide the Services described in the Service Schedule in accordance with this Agreement.

### 3.2 Service Levels

The Provider will use reasonable endeavours to meet the service levels specified in the Service Schedule. Service level commitments apply only during Business Hours unless otherwise specified.

### 3.3 Changes to Services

The Provider may make changes to the Services that:
- (a) Are required by law or regulation;
- (b) Do not materially reduce the functionality or quality of the Services;
- (c) Are improvements or enhancements to the Services.

Material changes to Services require thirty (30) days written notice to the Client.

### 3.4 Third-Party Services

The Services may include or rely upon third-party products and services. The Provider is not responsible for failures or changes in third-party services beyond our reasonable control, but will use reasonable endeavours to minimise any impact on the Client.

## 4. CLIENT OBLIGATIONS

### 4.1 Cooperation

The Client agrees to:
- (a) Provide the Provider with timely access to personnel, systems, information, and premises as reasonably required for the provision of Services;
- (b) Designate a primary contact person with authority to make decisions on the Client's behalf;
- (c) Respond to Provider requests for information or approvals within reasonable timeframes;
- (d) Ensure all users comply with acceptable use policies and security requirements.

### 4.2 Minimum Standards

The Client acknowledges that the Services are designed for environments meeting the minimum standards specified in the Service Schedule. The Client agrees to:
- (a) Maintain hardware and software within supported versions;
- (b) Remediate identified security gaps within agreed timeframes;
- (c) Use only licensed software;
- (d) Not circumvent security controls implemented by the Provider.

### 4.3 Accuracy of Information

The Client warrants that all information provided to the Provider is accurate and complete. The Client must promptly notify the Provider of any changes to user numbers, locations, or other information that may affect the Services or Fees.

### 4.4 Backups and Data

While the Provider implements backup solutions as described in the Service Schedule, the Client remains responsible for:
- (a) Ensuring critical data is identified for backup;
- (b) Verifying data can be restored when test restores are performed;
- (c) Maintaining appropriate retention policies for legal and regulatory compliance.

## 5. FEES AND PAYMENT

### 5.1 Fees

The Client agrees to pay the Fees specified in the Service Schedule.

### 5.2 Invoicing

The Provider will invoice the Client:
- (a) Monthly in advance for recurring Services;
- (b) Upon completion or as otherwise agreed for project work;
- (c) Upon execution of this Agreement for one-time onboarding fees.

### 5.3 Payment Terms

Unless otherwise specified in the Service Schedule, payment is due within fourteen (14) days of invoice date. All amounts are in Australian Dollars and exclusive of GST unless stated otherwise.

### 5.4 GST

All Fees are exclusive of GST. The Client must pay GST on taxable supplies at the rate prevailing at the time of supply.

### 5.5 Late Payment

If payment is not received by the due date:
- (a) The Provider may charge interest at the rate of 2% per month on overdue amounts;
- (b) After fourteen (14) days overdue, the Provider may suspend Services upon seven (7) days written notice;
- (c) The Client is responsible for reasonable collection costs incurred by the Provider.

### 5.6 Price Reviews

The Provider may review Fees annually. Any increase will:
- (a) Be limited to the greater of CPI (Brisbane All Groups) or 5% per annum;
- (b) Take effect upon thirty (30) days written notice;
- (c) Apply from the next billing period after the notice period.

### 5.7 Disputes

If the Client disputes any invoice, the Client must:
- (a) Pay the undisputed portion by the due date;
- (b) Notify the Provider in writing of the disputed amount and reasons within seven (7) days of invoice;
- (c) Work in good faith with the Provider to resolve the dispute.

The Provider retains all Intellectual Property rights in:

The Provider grants the Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use Provider tools and documentation solely for the purpose of receiving the Services during the Term.

## 7. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 7.1 Obligations

Each party agrees to:
- (a) Keep the other party's Confidential Information confidential;
- (b) Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties except as permitted by this Agreement;
- (c) Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement;
- (d) Take reasonable measures to protect Confidential Information.

### 7.2 Permitted Disclosures

A party may disclose Confidential Information:
- (a) To employees, contractors, and advisers who need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations;
- (b) As required by law, regulation, or court order;
- (c) With the prior written consent of the disclosing party.

### 7.3 Survival

Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

---

## 8. PRIVACY AND DATA PROTECTION

### 8.1 Privacy Act Compliance

Both parties agree to comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles to the extent applicable to their activities under this Agreement.

### 8.2 Personal Information

The Provider will:
- (a) Collect, use, and disclose Personal Information only as necessary to provide the Services;
- (b) Take reasonable steps to protect Personal Information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access;
- (c) Not transfer Personal Information outside Australia without appropriate safeguards or Client consent;
- (d) Assist the Client to respond to access and correction requests.

### 8.3 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach involving the Client's Personal Information:
- (a) The Provider will notify the Client within forty-eight (48) hours of becoming aware of the breach;
- (b) The parties will cooperate to assess and respond to the breach;
- (c) The Provider will assist with any notifications required under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme.

### 8.4 Data Location

Unless otherwise agreed, Client data will be stored in Australia or in jurisdictions with adequate privacy protections.

## 9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 9.1 Exclusion of Consequential Loss

To the maximum extent permitted by law, neither party is liable to the other for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including loss of profits, revenue, data, or business opportunities, even if advised of the possibility of such damages.

### 9.2 Liability Cap

Subject to clause 9.3, the Provider's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement is limited to the greater of:
- (a) The total Fees paid by the Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim; or
- (b) Fifty thousand dollars ($50,000).

### 9.3 Unlimited Liability

Nothing in this Agreement limits either party's liability for:
- (a) Death or personal injury caused by negligence;
- (b) Fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
- (c) Breach of confidentiality obligations;
- (d) Any liability that cannot be limited by law.

### 9.4 Mitigation

Each party must take reasonable steps to mitigate any loss or damage suffered.

### 9.5 Time Limit

Any claim under this Agreement must be brought within twelve (12) months of the claimant becoming aware of the circumstances giving rise to the claim.

## 10. TERMINATION

### 10.1 Termination for Convenience

After the Initial Term, either party may terminate this Agreement by providing ninety (90) days written notice.

### 10.2 Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other party:
- (a) Commits a material breach that is not remedied within thirty (30) days of written notice;
- (b) Becomes insolvent, enters administration, or has a receiver appointed;
- (c) Ceases or threatens to cease carrying on business.

### 10.3 Termination by Provider

The Provider may terminate this Agreement or suspend Services immediately if:
- (a) The Client fails to pay undisputed invoices for more than thirty (30) days after the due date;
- (b) The Client's use of Services poses a security risk to the Provider or other clients;
- (c) The Client breaches acceptable use policies.

### 10.4 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:
- (a) All rights granted to the Client under this Agreement cease;
- (b) The Client must pay all Fees for Services provided up to the termination date;
- (c) Early termination during the Initial Term may incur fees as specified in the Service Schedule.

---

## 11. TRANSITION ASSISTANCE

### 11.1 Transition Period

Upon expiry or termination of this Agreement, the Provider will provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of up to thirty (30) days to facilitate an orderly transition to a replacement provider or in-house management.

### 11.2 Transition Services

Transition assistance includes:
- (a) Providing copies of Client documentation maintained by the Provider;
- (b) Exporting Client data in standard formats;
- (c) Removing Provider tools and agents from Client systems;
- (d) Answering reasonable questions from the replacement provider.

### 11.3 Transition Fees

Transition assistance is provided at the Provider's then-current hourly rates unless termination is due to Provider breach.

---

## 12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 12.1 Negotiation

The parties agree to attempt to resolve any dispute arising under this Agreement through good faith negotiation between senior representatives within fourteen (14) days of written notice of the dispute.

### 12.2 Mediation

If negotiation is unsuccessful, the parties agree to participate in mediation administered by the Resolution Institute (or successor body) before commencing legal proceedings. The costs of mediation will be shared equally.

### 12.3 Urgent Relief

Nothing in this clause prevents either party from seeking urgent interlocutory relief from a court of competent jurisdiction.

### 12.4 Continuation of Services

Unless otherwise agreed, the Provider will continue to provide Services during any dispute resolution process, provided the Client continues to pay undisputed Fees.

## 13. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 13.1 Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of Queensland, Australia. The parties submit to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Queensland.

### 13.2 Notices

Notices under this Agreement must be in writing and delivered by:
- (a) Email to the address specified in the Service Schedule; or
- (b) Post to the address specified in this Agreement.

Notices are deemed received:
- (a) If by email, when sent unless the sender receives a delivery failure notification;
- (b) If by post, three (3) Business Days after posting.

### 13.3 Assignment

Neither party may assign or transfer this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, except that the Provider may assign to an affiliate or successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets.

### 13.4 Subcontracting

The Provider may subcontract elements of the Services to qualified third parties, provided the Provider remains responsible for the performance of the Services and ensures subcontractors are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations.

### 13.5 Force Majeure

Neither party is liable for failure to perform obligations (other than payment obligations) due to circumstances beyond reasonable control, including natural disasters, war, terrorism, pandemic, or government action.

### 13.6 Waiver

A waiver of any right under this Agreement is only effective if in writing and applies only to the specific instance.

### 13.7 Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.

### 13.8 Relationship

The parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

### 13.9 Survival

Clauses that by their nature should survive termination (including confidentiality, limitation of liability, and dispute resolution) survive expiry or termination of this Agreement.
